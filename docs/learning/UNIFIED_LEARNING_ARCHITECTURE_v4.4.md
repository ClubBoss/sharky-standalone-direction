## Core Product Thesis

The app should not feel like a poker textbook or solver trainer.

It should feel like a friendly daily skill game that quietly turns a beginner
into a stronger player.

Every world must answer four questions:

1. What new mental model does the learner gain?
2. What emotional win does the learner feel?
3. What real-table behavior changes?
4. How will the app make this habit-forming without adding noise?

## Non-Negotiables

1. One world, one cognitive shift.
2. One micro-session, one skill atom.
3. Teach before asking.
4. Table-first decisions beat explanation walls.
5. Mistakes become repair loops, not punishment.
6. No solver-first language in beginner worlds.
7. No infinite AI content.
8. Sharky is a curated mascot coach, not a chatbot.
9. Habit loops reinforce real mastery, not raw clicking.
10. Future worlds can be visible, but only honest playable worlds should launch.

## World Quality Contract

Every playable world needs these layers:

| Layer | Job |
| --- | --- |
| Cognitive shift | The new way of thinking the learner gains. |
| Emotional win | The simple human feeling that makes the world memorable. |
| Core atoms | The measurable table behaviors taught in the world. |
| Micro-sessions | 5-10 curated sessions, each with one atom. |
| Variations | Enough repeated spots to stabilize the atom. |
| Repair | Common mistakes and one short correction path. |
| Transfer | A 30-60 second real-play observation or checklist. |
| Mastery seed | A future Tier 2 / Tier 3 version can reuse the same atoms. |

## Canonical World Ladder

### Starter / World 0 - Poker from Zero

Cognitive shift:

- The table is not scary; it is readable.

Emotional win:

- "I know what is happening at the table."

Core atoms:

- hero and opponents
- seats and action order
- BTN / SB / BB
- stack, pot, blind posting
- card ranks and suits
- hole cards versus board
- street order: preflop, flop, turn, river
- hand rankings as rules literacy
- showdown and winning the pot
- basic action words: fold, check, call, raise

Transfer:

- Watch three hands and point to BTN, blinds, pot, and current actor.

Do not include:

- strategy claims
- charts
- range language
- initiative theory

### World 1 - Hand Discipline

Cognitive shift:

- Not every hand deserves play.

Emotional win:

- "I can save chips by folding bad spots."

Core atoms:

- premium / strong / medium / trash buckets
- weak ace awareness
- dominated hand awareness
- fold discipline
- continue versus let go
- hand strength before ego

Transfer:

- Watch five starting hands and label which ones are easy folds.

Do not include:

- full preflop charts
- exact combo math
- position theory as the main lesson

### World 2 - Position Thinking

Cognitive shift:

- Position changes hand value and comfort.

Emotional win:

- "I understand why the Button feels powerful."

Core atoms:

- UTG / HJ / CO / BTN / SB / BB recognition
- early versus late
- in position versus out of position
- BTN advantage
- same hand, different seat, different action
- acting last postflop

Transfer:

- Watch one orbit and identify who acts last after the flop.

Do not include:

- range theory
- deep initiative
- board texture as first-time material

### World 3 - Preflop Framework

Cognitive shift:

- Preflop decisions have a simple structure.

Emotional win:

- "I have a calm first decision."

Core atoms:

- first-in unopened pot
- open or fold
- facing an open
- call or fold
- simple raise meaning
- bucket + seat + action frame
- no chart memorization

Transfer:

- Before looking at the result, name whether the pot is unopened or someone
  already opened.

Do not include:

- solver charts
- advanced 3-bet trees
- dominated-hand theory as new material

### World 4 - Bet Purpose And Price

Cognitive shift:

- A bet has a job and creates a price.

Emotional win:

- "I know why chips go in."

Core atoms:

- value wants weaker calls
- bluff wants folds
- protection denies free cards
- small / half-pot / pot-size pressure
- facing a price
- call small price, fold bad price
- purpose before size

Transfer:

- Watch a hand and label one bet as value, bluff, or protection attempt.

Do not include:

- fine sizing trees
- minimum defense
- formula-heavy pot odds

### World 5 - Board And Draws

Cognitive shift:

- The board can matter more than your private cards.

Emotional win:

- "I can read what changed."

Core atoms:

- dry versus wet board
- connected versus disconnected board
- obvious straight draw
- obvious flush draw
- outs as improvement cards
- turn changes texture
- river completes or misses draws
- board can weaken a good hand

Transfer:

- Pause a real hand on the flop and say whether the board is dry or wet.

Do not include:

- blocker theory
- precise equity math
- combo counting

### World 6 - Range Thinking Lite

Cognitive shift:

- Players can have groups of hands, not one exact hand.

Emotional win:

- "I can think in buckets, not guesses."

Core atoms:

- strong / medium / weak / missed range buckets
- who has more strong hands
- board fit intuition
- pressure from represented strength
- bet more / check more as simplified output

Transfer:

- After a flop, ask who has more strong hands before seeing the action.

Do not include:

- frequencies
- combinatorics
- solver charts

### World 7 - Stack Depth And Risk

Cognitive shift:

- Stack depth changes the cost of mistakes.

Emotional win:

- "I know when a decision is expensive."

Core atoms:

- 100 BB versus 20 BB
- commitment intuition
- shallow stack pressure
- jam/fold intuition without tables
- risk control

Transfer:

- Before a hand starts, label stacks as deep, medium, or short.

### World 8 - Tournament Pressure

Cognitive shift:

- Survival pressure can change good poker decisions.

Emotional win:

- "I understand why tournaments feel different."

Core atoms:

- bubble pressure
- payout jumps
- survival value
- risk premium intuition
- short-stack urgency

Transfer:

- In a tournament hand, notice whether survival pressure is present.

### World 9 - Player Adjustment

Cognitive shift:

- Real opponents are not identical.

Emotional win:

- "I can adjust one lever to one player."

Core atoms:

- loose versus tight
- passive versus aggressive
- one-lever adjustment
- do not level yourself
- exploit-lite, not solver exploit

Transfer:

- Tag one opponent as loose/tight or passive/aggressive, then stop.

### World 10 - Real Play Transfer / Capstone

Cognitive shift:

- Training transfers into a simple real-play routine.

Emotional win:

- "I can sit down with a plan."

Core atoms:

- pre-session checklist
- table observation
- first orbit discipline
- mistake review
- hand note habit
- capstone mixed session

Transfer:

- Play or watch a short session and record one hand, one mistake, and one good
  fold.

## Micro-Session Contract

A strong micro-session should feel like a small journey:

1. Hook: one short reason this matters.
2. Show: table state demonstrates the atom.
3. Guided action: one low-risk decision.
4. Variation: same atom, slightly changed surface.
5. Repair: if wrong, show the table reason.
6. Prove: one short recall or checkpoint.
7. Reward: XP, Sharky reaction, or progress signal tied to mastery.

Target size:

- 3-7 minutes
- 6-12 decisions when the session is interactive
- 1 core atom
- 1 supporting cue at most

## Anti-Boredom Rhythm

Do not let five tasks in a row feel identical.

Rotate interaction types after the base concept is stable:

- tap a seat
- choose an action
- classify a board
- compare two hands
- spot the mistake
- predict the next actor
- replay a short action trail
- quick recap

Rule:

- variety supports mastery; it must not hide confusion.

## Sharky Role

Sharky is the emotional layer, not the content engine.

Allowed:

- one pre-session line
- one short reaction after correct or wrong outcome
- one identity reinforcement after meaningful progress
- light encouragement during repair

Not allowed:

- open-ended chat
- generated coaching
- repeated interruptions
- jokes that hide the poker lesson

Voice:

- warm
- concise
- specific
- never shaming

## Reward And Habit Contract

Rewards should reinforce useful behavior:

- clean pass
- corrected mistake
- streak of thoughtful correct answers
- completed world
- returned for daily practice
- improved weak spot

Rewards should not reinforce:

- spam tapping
- skipping explanations
- grinding meaningless XP

## Release-v1 Content Bar

Release v1 should not pretend the whole academy is complete.

Minimum launch-quality playable spine:

- Starter / W0 dense and polished
- W1 Hand Discipline playable
- W2 Position Thinking playable
- W3 Preflop Framework playable
- W4 Bet Purpose And Price playable
- W5 Board And Draws at least a believable bridge or clearly locked preview
- W6-W10 visible as honest future ladder unless dense

## Upgrade Path From v4.3.1

Keep:

- one world equals one cognitive shift
- no solver-first foundation
- deterministic content
- finite core path

Improve:

- add emotional win per world
- add transfer task per world
- separate Starter / W0 from W1 Hand Discipline in product planning
- move initiative/range concepts later
- turn every playable world into intro/practice/apply/review, not just a label
- add anti-boredom rhythm and mastery tier hooks
