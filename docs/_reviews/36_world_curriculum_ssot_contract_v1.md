# 36-World Curriculum SSOT Contract v1

## 1. Verdict

`ssot_ready_no_code`

This contract resolves status language without changing the Master Plan, active
content, runtime, entitlement state, or historical calibration. It defines how
to describe the current route and the planned W1-W36 curriculum together.

## 2. Authority precedence

For **current learner-access status**, use evidence in this order:

1. Current runtime route entry and active route dispatch.
2. Canonical campaign/session registration backing that entry.
3. Focused route test evidence, including its current pass/fail state.
4. Active-root authored shelf and manifest/session files.
5. Current planning documents for intended route shape and safe surface policy.
6. Historical calibration for quality assessment and historical intent.
7. Long-term curriculum vision for world theme, conceptual sequence, and later
   frontier direction.

Higher evidence wins only for the claim it proves. A content shelf does not
prove learner-visible access. A route test does not prove commercial access or
course completeness. Historical calibration may describe quality or intention;
it cannot override active-root route truth. Planned curriculum names cannot
imply playability.

## 3. Canonical status vocabulary

| Status | Required evidence | Allowed wording | Forbidden wording | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `route_verified` | runtime entry, canonical registration, passing focused route proof without relevant baseline residue | `Available in the current route` | `complete course`, `premium unlocked` | Use only after all required route proof is green. |
| `campaign_routed` | runtime entry plus canonical campaign/session registration; route tests may exist with documented baseline residue | `Current campaign continuation` | `release-grade`, `fully verified on every surface` | W7-W10 today. |
| `authored_not_routed` | active-root shelf/manifest exists, no route entry evidence | `Authored and not yet in the learner route` | `playable`, `available now` | A future shelf before campaign registration. |
| `planned_foundation` | current curriculum/Volume I planning definition, no route proof | `Planned foundation` / `Coming later` | `finish now`, `unlocked` | W11-W12 today. |
| `planned_frontier` | long-term W13-W24 curriculum policy | `Later strategic depth` | `available through upgrade`, `ready for specialization` | W13-W24. |
| `deferred_specialization` | W25-W36 specialization/pro policy | `Later advanced frontier` | `full Cash/MTT track available` | W25-W36. |
| `missing_truth` | evidence is absent or sources conflict without resolvable precedence | `Not yet verified` | any availability or quality claim | A world with no shelf, plan, or route evidence. |

`release-strong` is a calibration band, not a current learner-access status.
It may appear as a qualified historical quality note only.

## 5. Volume I wording contract

Safe statements:

- `W1-W10 form the current route-backed portion of the shared foundation.`
- `W1-W12 is the intended shared Volume I foundation.`
- `W11-W12 are planned foundation chapters, not current route destinations.`
- `W13+ is a later frontier; the W12 -> W13 gateway is not active.`

Forbidden statements:

- `Complete W1-W12 is playable.`
- `Finish Volume I now.`
- `Unlock W13 through upgrade.`
- `You are specialization ready.`
- `Full Cash/MTT track available.`
- `AI/adaptive mastery path.`
- `Leaks resurfacing is live.`

The W1-W4 free / W5+ future paid-depth boundary remains a strategic boundary,
not proof of an active paywall, entitlement, unlock, price, or trial.

## 6. Historical calibration reconciliation

Read older `release-strong W1-W12` calibration as a quality reference and a
record of intended ladder strength at the time of that review. It is not:

- current runtime status;
- current entitlement or access status;
- current route-test proof for W11/W12; or
- permission to represent W1-W12 as complete or W13 as accessible.

Where calibration and active-root evidence diverge, retain the calibration as
historical quality context and use current route evidence for product claims.

## 7. Surface implications

Future Learn/Home surfaces may show W7-W10 as playable campaign continuation,
with no broad completion or commercial claim. They may show W11-W12 as `Coming
later` / `Planned foundation`, and W13+ only as a compact later frontier. Do
not render a W12 -> W13 gateway until W11/W12 receive active shelves,
canonical route ownership, learner entries, route tests, and a separately
admitted readiness contract. No status label may imply premium access,
commerce, trial, mastery, leaks resurfacing, or AI adaptation.
