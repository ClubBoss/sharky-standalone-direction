## 5. Home Findings

Home is commercially clear. The first viewport says `Today's table read`, names the active skill family `Fold, check, call, raise`, and gives a strong `Continue` CTA. The short table practice card explains the loop: learn, practice, review, keep sharp.

Density is medium because the four-step practice checklist is visible, but it supports the product story rather than distracting from it.

## 6. Practice Pre-Completion Findings

Practice pre-completion is ready. `Quick daily drill`, `3 spots`, `~3 min`, and `Start daily set` make the job obvious. The state avoids paywall pressure and keeps the daily trainer concrete.

The skill pack grid is visible below the primary task without taking over the first action.

## 7. Practice Completion / Result Ceremony Findings

The completed Practice state is one of the strongest commercial proofs. `Daily table trainer complete`, `Done for today`, `Practice extra reps`, and the `Session complete` result card create a truthful completion moment.

The ceremony is premium enough for current first-week proof: not cinematic, but clear, calm, and tied to one table clue warming up.

## 8. Correct Feedback Findings

Correct feedback passes. The table is the visual hero, `Good read` is the first trainer beat, `No bet yet` is visible on the table and in the feedback row, and `Best play: Check` connects the signal to the action.

This is Sharky's strongest Runout-answering moment: user choice -> visible table signal -> why -> next same-clue rep.

## 9. Wrong Feedback Findings

Wrong feedback passes. `Not quite` is clear without feeling punitive, the missed clue is `No bet yet`, and `Better option: Check` is easy to understand.

The repair copy is compact and beginner-safe. It does not become report-like or solver-like.

## 10. Review Findings

Review open repair is commercially credible. It shows exactly one fix waiting, compares `You chose` vs `Better`, explains why, and gives a direct `Repair this clue` CTA.

Exact repaired/clean Review proof remains deferred, but open-repair proof is enough for this first-week packet because the active repair loop is legible.

## 11. Learn Findings

Learn is clear and polished. It shows World 1 progress, the current mission, the specific current step, and a large `Start` CTA. The journey preview explains the first week without becoming a dashboard.

Density is medium, but it is organized and scan-friendly.

## 12. Profile Findings

Profile communicates rhythm and proof. Level, streak, XP, completed tasks, milestone, and progress proof are visible in the first viewport.

This surface is slightly more information-dense than the strongest screens, but it does not block commercial readiness.
