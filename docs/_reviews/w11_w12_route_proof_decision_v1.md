## 4. Recommended decision

Admit **W11 route-proof slice** next. Keep W12 planned.

W11 has enough dormant lesson/runner source intent to define a narrow admission
target, while W12 is the end-of-Volume-I decision-quality/mindset world whose
premature routing could be misread as Volume I completion or a W13 handoff.

## 5. Proof requirements if admitted

W11 may become `campaign_routed` only after all of the following exist:

1. Active-root W11 shelf and authored source owned by the current content
   system.
2. Canonical W11 campaign/session registration.
3. Learner-visible entry from the current route without replacing Act0 Home.
4. Focused deterministic W11 route and entry tests.
5. Safe status wording: current W11 continuation only; no W12 completion,
   W13 gateway, paid unlock, mastery, AI, leak, or specialization claim.
6. Explicit proof that the next unavailable boundary remains W12 planned and
   W13+ frontier-only.

## 6. Scope boundary for future implementation

The recommended slice is **one world: W11**. It may add the minimum active
content source, registration, learner entry, and focused tests needed to prove
W11. It must not add W12, change W12 status, add a W12-to-W13 gateway, or
expand broad content beyond the admitted W11 proof path.

Forbidden changes: paywall/trial/price/entitlement work, commerce UI,
specialization, AI/adaptive/mastery/leak claims, Modern Table changes, Home
redesign, external packaging, and unrelated content migration.

## 7. Surface / monetization implications

If W11 becomes routed, the Volume I surface may describe W1-W11 as the current
route-backed portion only after proof passes; W12 remains `Planned foundation`
and W13+ remains later frontier. The W1-W4 free / W5+ future paid-depth
boundary remains strategic only. W11 routing creates neither a paid unlock nor
a W13 access claim.
