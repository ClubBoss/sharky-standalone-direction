# Volume I Visible Depth Plan v1

## 1. Verdict

`plan_ready_with_surface_contract`

## 2. Current truth

Volume I is the shared `W1-W12` foundation: it takes a true beginner to
independently competent early play before specialization. `W13-W24` is later
applied postflop and strategic depth; `W25-W36` is the advanced specialization
and professional frontier. The long-term vision is a planning anchor, not a
current build commitment, and it explicitly defers W13+ authoring until W12 is
release-grade.

The accepted first-week proof is narrower and real: placement routes into one
table decision, a visible signal explains the result, and a miss can produce a
repair/recheck continuation through Home, Review, and Profile. W5 board-texture
and W6 range-bucket records now make a small classifier repair seam auditable;
they do not make mastery, a full course, or a visible academy surface.

Current authored proof is strongest through W1-W6. W7-W12 have an authoritative
curriculum definition but must not be presented as playable without a separate
content/route verification. UI, commerce, a paywall, full 36-world production,
and external packaging remain deferred.

## 3. Product risk

First-week proof can make Sharky feel useful yet small if the learner cannot
see a credible route beyond the immediate rep. Runout appears broader because
it exposes a large academy/library hierarchy. Sharky should answer that risk
with an honest finite route and concrete learning loops, not copied categories,
chapter counts, or a content encyclopedia. The failure to avoid is a "small
proof app" impression; the equal failure is pretending that planned worlds are
currently playable or paid depth is currently purchasable.

## 4. Proposed visible depth model

The future Learn surface should show one finite route with explicit state:

1. `Volume I — Shared Foundation (W1-W12)`: one common beginner-to-competent
   spine, with each world labelled playable only where route/content truth
   proves it.
2. `Volume II — Later Strategic Depth (W13-W24)`: a locked future frontier for
   applied postflop, range, and strategic depth; no specialization promise or
   access claim yet.
3. `Volume III — Advanced Frontier (W25-W36)`: a locked later frontier for
   eventual specialist and advanced-study paths.

Each landmark must distinguish `playable now`, `planned foundation`, and
`later frontier`. W1-W4 remain the free public foundation and W5+ remains a
future paid-depth boundary; neither statement authorizes an unlock, price,
trial, or premium CTA.

## 6. W13+ locked preview rule

Safe preview: show only a compact, clearly locked `Later strategic depth
(W13-W24)` landmark and a compact `Advanced frontier (W25-W36)` landmark. Use
copy such as `Planned next: deeper postflop and strategic study` and `Later:
advanced specialist paths`.

Keep these states locked/coming later. Do not claim they are playable, complete,
available with an upgrade, personalized, solver-backed, Cash/MTT-ready, or
scheduled. Do not show prices, trials, purchase language, or a world-unlock
promise. The preview is a route horizon, not a commerce surface.

## 7. Depth without Runout cloning

Sharky's depth model is a finite learning route: table signal -> decision ->
why -> repair/recheck -> later mixed recall and leak resurfacing when those
systems are actually owned. It is not 280 visible chapter rows, an encyclopedia,
or a copied competitor taxonomy. The competitive benchmark is the learner job:
make future depth legible without displacing Sharky's value-before-paywall and
deterministic table-signal proof.

## 8. Surface contract, not UI implementation

A future surface may show the current playable world, the next planned
foundation landmark, locked later frontiers, and only already-owned evidence of
reps or repair loops. It must label states honestly, keep one dominant forward
route, and preserve value before any paywall boundary. It must not create a
dashboard, infer skill atoms as learner-visible mastery, invent mixed recall,
or convert future paid depth into a current entitlement state.
