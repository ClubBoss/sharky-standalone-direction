# Runout Onboarding And Paywall Notes

## Evidence Used

- XAPK manifest: billing, notifications, ad services, install referrer.
- APK assets: onboarding welcome/background assets, how-it-works images, improvement-question assets, paywall backgrounds, discount backgrounds, paywall video.
- Bundle taxonomy: onboarding, improve, calibration, subscription, premium, trial, paywall token families.

## First Promise / Positioning

- Runout appears to package itself as a complete poker-improvement system rather than a narrow lesson runner.
- The asset taxonomy suggests an early promise around improving poker skill through guided training, daily sessions, analytics, and skill mastery.
- Premium presentation is a major part of the first impression: custom fonts, branded backgrounds, glass-like surfaces, and poker-specific imagery.

## Calibration / Onboarding Flow Clues

- Onboarding and recalibration terms imply the product asks about user state and can later retune the trainer.
- Question/background assets imply a visually authored onboarding sequence, not a plain form.
- "How it works" assets imply the app explains its learning mechanism before or around first use.

## Personalization Claims

- Signals point to personalization through trainer calibration, skill drill selection, player profile/rating, session length, formats, and venue/game-type preferences.
- Risk: if the UI does not show exactly why the route changed, personalization can feel like a black box.

## Subscription / Paywall Packaging Clues

- Billing client marker and billing permission confirm subscription/purchase infrastructure.
- Paywall, discount, card background, and paywall video assets indicate a mature commercial surface.
- Trial/discount/premium token families suggest upgrade packaging is integrated into app flow.

## What Sharky Should Borrow As Principle

- Make onboarding feel premium and authored, not like setup.
- Give the user an immediate sense that the product has a plan.
- Show a compact "how this works" proof before asking for deeper commitment.
- Use visuals to sell seriousness, but make table proof the trust engine.
- Let calibration feel personal, but prove it through deterministic route evidence.

## What Sharky Must Avoid

- Do not show paywall/trial pressure before real learning proof.
- Do not copy Runout wording, asset structure, video treatment, backgrounds, or layout.
- Do not add broad onboarding questions unless every answer visibly affects the first route.
- Do not claim AI/adaptive personalization unless Sharky can show the rule or signal.
- Do not let commercial packaging outrun beginner trust.

## Sharky Response

Best near-term response: keep Act0 placement short, then make the first hand answer prove personalization and learning value through a visible table signal. Monetization should remain downstream of first value.
