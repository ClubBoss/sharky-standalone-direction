## Current Calibration

Practical product state today:

- product route estimate: `~81 / 100`
- strongest area: `Content and world readiness`
- strong areas: `Home`, `Table/Result`, `Review`
- weakest active product areas:
  - `Play` quick-return usefulness
  - `Sharky / rewards / habit`
  - `Visual and premium feel`
  - `Placement` final trust / aha closure
  - `Profile` identity clarity
  - `Value / trial` value-after-proof timing and implementation reality

Important distinction:

- this route is for practical product `100 / 100`
- it is not the same as final store/distribution `100 / 100`
- launch/store readiness still remains governed by
  `PROJECT_READINESS_EPICS_SSOT_v1.md`

## Route Principles

1. One active bottleneck family at a time.
2. Prefer user-visible leaps over hidden system expansion.
3. Do not reopen already-strong surfaces unless they directly block the next
   wave.
4. Keep `Play`, `Sharky`, `Placement`, `Review`, and `You` subordinate to the
   canonical loop:
   `Placement -> Home -> Learn -> Table -> Result -> Home`
5. Keep value/trial strictly value-after-proof.
6. Treat simplicity as a guardrail on every wave, not as a late cleanup excuse.

## Wave Order

### Wave 1. Play As Quick-Return Practice Layer

Goal:
Turn `Play` into the clearest fast-return surface in the product.

In scope:

- one dominant `Today's Drill` or equivalent quick-return entry
- deterministic quick-practice ordering
- short time-to-complete visibility
- clear tie-back from practice to course progress

Success signal:

- `Play` is clearly not a second `Learn`
- the learner can enter useful practice in one tap
- practice feels short, purposeful, and habit-friendly

Must not do:

- no expansion into broad new game modes
- no duplicate world-map behavior
- no deep rewards layer in this wave

### Wave 2. Visual Proof And Shell Polish

Goal:
Raise the whole active shell to honest external-review quality.

In scope:

- screenshot/device pass across `Placement`, `Home`, `Learn`, `Play`,
  `Review`, `You`, `Table`, and `Result`
- CTA hierarchy cleanup
- spacing, safe-area, overflow, empty-state, and consistency fixes
- minimal high-value motion/polish where clearly missing

Success signal:

- the active shell reads as one coherent premium product
- no obvious visual debt remains on compact phone, large phone, and tablet

Must not do:

- no broad redesign of dormant systems
- no isolated "pretty" polish that weakens route clarity

### Wave 3. Sharky, Earned Rewards, And Habit Energy

Goal:
Make the product feel alive without drifting into noisy gamification or AI chat.

In scope:

- compact curated Sharky phrase set
- visible streak-lite or return cue
- earned celebrations tied to real learning proof
- lightweight daily-return hook aligned with `Home` and `Play`

Success signal:

- the product gains emotional continuity
- rewards reinforce learning progress instead of competing with it

Must not do:

- no deep persona/AI expansion
- no leaderboard, vanity, or fake-pressure loops

### Wave 4. Placement Trust And Micro-Aha Closure

Goal:
Make first start feel short, confident, and personally useful.

In scope:

- tighter placement flow
- one clear "you're in the right place" moment
- first-route handoff quality
- removal of pre-value premium pressure from placement if still present

Success signal:

- placement feels like trusted routing, not setup work
- the user exits placement with one obvious next step and early confidence

Must not do:

- no long diagnostic funnel
- no monetization push before first useful learning proof

### Wave 5. Review Pattern Coaching And You Identity Mirror

Goal:
Convert progress from isolated events into visible learner growth.

In scope:

- group repeated mistakes into useful pattern coaching where evidence supports it
- keep `Review` focused on one repair path at a time
- reshape `You` into a compact identity mirror:
  strengths, weak spots, next focus, earned growth

Success signal:

- `Review` feels like a coach, not a log
- `You` answers: "What kind of player am I becoming?"

Must not do:

- no dashboard bloat
- no taxonomy-heavy review browser

### Wave 6. Value/Trial Closure And Final Product Pass

Goal:
Close the remaining practical product gap around premium timing, trust, and
final shell coherence.

In scope:

- real value-first premium/trial timing
- concrete free/premium boundary behavior
- purchase/restore/trial truth if this route is admitted now
- final copy/trust sweep
- final simplicity/performance boundary pass on active Act0 route

Success signal:

- premium feels like expansion after proof
- no major system-noise copy remains on active learner-facing surfaces
- active route remains cheap enough to evolve

Must not do:

- no store/distribution packaging takeover
- no full-repo simplification crusade outside the active route

## Cross-Wave Guardrails

These rules apply on every wave:

1. Do not reopen dormant persona / AI-coach systems as product owners.
2. Do not move `Play` or `Review` into competition with `Learn`.
3. Do not treat broad code deletion as product progress unless it strengthens
   the active Act0 route directly.
4. Do not close any wave on code confidence alone where device or human proof is
   required.
5. Do not advance premium pressure ahead of learner trust.

## Definition Of 100 For This Route

Practical product `100 / 100` is reached when all of the following are true at
the same time:

1. `Placement` is short, trustful, and ends with one clear route handoff.
2. `Home` and `Play` together create an obvious return loop.
3. `Learn` feels like a real journey, not a shelf.
4. `Table -> Result` feels polished, readable, and emotionally rewarding.
5. `Review` repairs patterns, not only isolated mistakes.
6. `You` mirrors identity and improvement without dashboard bloat.
7. `Sharky`, streak-lite, and celebrations add soul without noise.
8. Premium/trial appears only after proven value and feels additive.
9. The active shell survives real-device visual review cleanly.
10. The product remains simple enough that the next wave is still cheap to ship.

## Verification Rhythm

Minimum proof per wave:

- targeted tests for the touched learner-facing seam
- `flutter analyze`
- `./tools/fast_loop_world1_v1.sh` when the active route is touched broadly
- `git diff --check`
- screenshot / device proof when the wave claims visual closure
- human walkthrough when the wave claims trust, aha, or coaching-quality closure
