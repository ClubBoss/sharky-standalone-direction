## Audit Order

Run these audits in order. Do not skip ahead unless a prior audit is blocked by
non-product infrastructure noise.

### Audit A. Device / UI Proof

Goal:
Prove that the active learner path survives real device/screenshot review
cleanly enough to stop treating visual quality as an unverified assumption.

Surfaces:

- `Placement`
- `Home`
- `Learn`
- `Play`
- `Review`
- `You`
- `Table`
- `Result`

Required form factors:

- compact phone
- large phone
- tablet

What to collect:

- only real user-visible defects
- safe-area, overflow, density, spacing, hierarchy, and empty-state residue
- only one screenshot set per decisive state; do not build a gallery for its
  own sake

What does not count:

- simulator/bootstrap noise that does not reflect the active product UI
- abstract design preferences without route or readability impact
- speculative redesign ideas

Exit criteria:

1. each required surface is checked on admitted device classes
2. any found defects collapse into one bounded polish task family
3. no major CTA hierarchy, overflow, or layout incoherence remains on the
   active route

### Audit B. Novice Walkthrough Proof

Goal:
Prove that the first-user route is actually clear and useful to a novice, not
just internally coherent to builders.

Minimum proof set:

- 2-3 bounded first-user runs
- first session only
- focus on the active route, not dormant systems

Execution protocol:

- use `docs/plan/NOVICE_WALKTHROUGH_PROTOCOL_v1.md`
- keep the route bounded to:
  `Placement -> Home -> first useful hand -> Result -> back to Home`

Questions to answer:

1. Does the learner understand where they are and what happens next?
2. Does `Placement` feel like trusted routing rather than setup work?
3. Does the first useful rep create a real "aha" moment?
4. Do early worlds make the next decision feel more understandable?
5. Before or at first start, does the learner understand what this app is,
   why it works, and why starting now is worth the effort?
6. If login, account, or first-start gating exists, does it support trust
   rather than delay value?

What to record:

- confusion points
- hesitation points
- misleading copy
- missing reassurance
- moments where "why am I doing this?" is not answered clearly enough
- places where pre-entry orientation, motivation, or first-start explanation
  is missing badly enough to reduce conversion into the first useful action

Exit criteria:

1. first-session clarity is confirmed by bounded walkthrough evidence
2. first-session aha is confirmed or reduced to one bounded polish family
3. no major trust break remains in the route from `Placement` into the first
   useful action loop
4. if pre-entry explanation or login/first-start framing is a real blocker, it
   is reduced to one bounded owner family instead of being left as a vague note

### Audit C. Feedback Residue Sweep

Goal:
Remove the last obvious generic feedback language that keeps the route from
feeling precise and coaching-grade.

Primary targets:

- repeated generic titles such as `Nice read.` and `Almost there.`
- generic reaction copy on `Play`, `Result`, and repair-adjacent feedback
- any learner-visible feedback that sounds system-shaped instead of
  scenario-first

What to preserve:

- deterministic behavior
- current feedback floor protections
- calm learner-facing tone

Exit criteria:

1. the dominant generic feedback families are either removed or materially
   reduced
2. learner-visible feedback stays specific enough to teach the scenario
3. the sweep stays inside the active route and does not reopen content-sprawl
