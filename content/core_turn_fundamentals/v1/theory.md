# Turn Fundamentals

## Key Idea
- The turn is often the most important decision street after the flop.
- Only one card remains, so mistakes cost more and plans must be clearer.
- Good turn play comes from board change, position, and whether your hand wants a big or small pot.

## Definitions
- Definition: A brick turn is a card that does not change much about the board.
- Definition: A dynamic turn is a card that adds strong draws or changes who likely has the best hand.

## Mini-Example
- Flop is `K72` rainbow and the preflop raiser bets small, then gets called.
- Turn is an Ace. This card usually helps the raiser more than the caller.
- A larger turn bet can now pressure medium hands and charge draws.

## Working Rules
- Bet more often when the turn helps your likely strong hands.
- Check more often when the turn helps the caller and your hand is medium.
- On brick turns, smaller bets can keep weaker hands in and protect your range.
- On dynamic turns, larger bets can deny equity and build value with strong hands.
- In position, use the extra information to control pot size with medium-strength hands.
