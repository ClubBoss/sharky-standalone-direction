# Bankroll Management

## Key Idea
- A bankroll is the money set aside for poker.
- Good bankroll rules keep one bad stretch from forcing poor decisions.
- The goal is stability: pick stakes, shot-taking rules, and stop-loss limits before you sit down.

## Mini-Example
- A player keeps 50 online cash buy-ins for a stake.
- After losing 3 buy-ins in one session, the player stops, reviews hands, and returns later with a clear head.
- If the bankroll drops below the planned threshold, the player moves down until the roll rebuilds.

## Working Rules
- Keep enough buy-ins for the game type and how swingy it is.
- Take a shot at a higher stake only when your bankroll is above the normal minimum.
- End a session when your planned stop-loss is reached.
- Track results by game and table type so stake decisions come from data, not mood.
- Prefer soft lineups and lower-rake games when the choice is close.

## Quick Check
- Why is moving down a bankroll decision instead of a failure?
- What changes when a format has bigger swings?
