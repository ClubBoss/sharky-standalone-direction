# Session w11.s01

## Objective
Use one pre-session focus to turn visible table information into one clear
action objective and one reviewable observation.

## Scenario
You are beginning a short table session. Across six planned reps, pause before
the decision and name one visible signal: position, player tendency, board
texture, price, draw quality, or showdown value. The price-first transfer
thread remains: re-check price before continuing with a draw whenever draw
quality is the decision.

### Scenario rep 1

Hero is Big Blind versus Button on a dry ace-high flop, has a weak hand with
no strong draw, and faces a Button bet around half pot. The focus is to fold
when position and board texture do not support hope.

### Scenario rep 2

Hero is Button versus Big Blind on a connected two-tone flop with an open-ended
straight draw. Big Blind leads around one-quarter pot. The focus is to continue
when position, price, and a real improvement path align.

### Scenario rep 3

Hero is Cutoff versus Big Blind on a paired two-tone board with no pair and
only weak backdoor potential. A straightforward Big Blind leads around
three-quarters pot. The focus is to fold rather than chase a weak draw at a bad
price.

### Scenario rep 4

Hero is Button versus Small Blind on a low dry flop with two overcards and
backdoor potential. Small Blind bluffs too often and bets around one-quarter
pot. The focus is to continue when player tendency and price make folding too
quickly a mistake.

### Scenario rep 5

Hero is Hijack versus Big Blind on a coordinated high-card flop with ace-high
and no strong draw. A tight Big Blind check-raises after Hero bets small. The
focus is to fold when player tendency and action both point to strength.

### Scenario rep 6

Hero is Button versus Big Blind on a semi-dry middle-card flop with middle
pair. A wide Big Blind bets around one-quarter pot. The focus is to continue
when position, price, and reasonable showdown value align.

## Decision
For every rep, identify one visible signal first, then choose the action that
matches the focus. When the price is not justified, folding is a successful
transfer decision. When continuing is justified, state the improvement you are
trying to make instead of calling by habit.

## Explanation
Real-play transfer is not a new theory layer. It is a repeatable sequence:
notice one signal, use an earlier concept, make one clear decision, and review
what happened. The correction is immediate: if the decision ignored the price,
recheck the price before the next similar spot. This makes earlier position,
board, draw, price, and action-purpose lessons usable under normal table pace.
