# Session w11.s01

## Objective
Use one pre-session focus to turn visible table information into one clear
action objective and one reviewable observation.

## Scenario
You are beginning a short table session. Your focus is to avoid calling a bad
price with a weak draw. Across six planned reps, pause before the decision and
name the visible signal: position, player tendency, board texture, or price.

1. Before the first meaningful pot, choose the single focus: notice the price
   before continuing with a draw.
2. A late-position player opens; identify your position before considering your
   hand strength.
3. A loose caller bets on a wet flop while you hold a weak draw; name the board
   texture and the price before deciding whether a call has a clear job.
4. You face a small price with a visible draw; state what improvement card you
   need before continuing.
5. You face a large price with the same draw; fold when the price no longer
   supports the attempt rather than chasing the last card.
6. After the session, record one hand where the price signal changed your
   decision and one signal to watch next time.

## Decision
For every rep, identify one visible signal first, then choose the action that
matches the focus. When the price is not justified, folding is a successful
transfer decision. When continuing is justified, state the improvement you are
trying to make instead of calling by habit.

## Explanation
Real-play transfer is not a new theory layer. It is a repeatable sequence:
notice one signal, use an earlier concept, make one clear decision, and review
what happened. The correction is immediate: if the decision ignored the price,
recheck the price before the next similar spot. This makes earlier position,
board, draw, price, and action-purpose lessons usable under normal table pace.
