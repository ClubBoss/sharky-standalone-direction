# Session w6.s01

## Objective
Build the first World 6 bridge by sorting likely hands into broad range buckets instead of staring at one hand in isolation.

A combo is one specific set of hole cards a player can hold.

## Scenario
Each rep shows a simple action frame and asks which broad hand bucket best fits the likely range:
strong,
medium,
weak,
or missed.
The goal is to notice that a decision starts from a set of likely hands, not from one exact combo.

## Decision
Given a hand category and board-fit cue, choose the broad range bucket that
best matches the situation.

## Explanation
World 6 starts when learners stop treating every spot like a single-hand puzzle.
Range buckets make likely holdings concrete enough to compare stronger and weaker hand families without jumping into combinatorics or solver detail.
