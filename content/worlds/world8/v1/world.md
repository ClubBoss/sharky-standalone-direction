# World 8

## Overview
- World 8 is the learner-facing home for bounded tournament and intuitive ICM thinking.
- This world teaches that chips do not keep the same practical value in every tournament spot:
  survival pressure changes what risks are reasonable,
  covering and being covered change who can push edges,
  and stack-preservation can matter more than raw chip EV.
- The goal is not solver-grade ICM precision or exact push-fold chart memorization. The goal is to make tournament pressure concrete enough that learners can recognize when survival, payout pressure, and stack interaction should change an otherwise familiar decision.
- This world comes after board reading, range thinking, and stack-depth basics. Once learners can evaluate a spot in chip-EV terms, the next step is understanding when tournament life and payout pressure change that evaluation.

## Goals
- Teach ICM buckets in plain language rather than dense payout math.
- Introduce risk premium as a practical learner-facing concept:
  sometimes continuing requires a stronger reason because busting carries extra cost.
- Show the difference between survival pressure and aggression pressure near the bubble and other payout-sensitive spots.
- Explain how covering versus being covered changes practical freedom to pressure or continue.
- Prepare learners for later tournament and exploit layers without turning this world into a solver-heavy or specialization-owned policy system.

## Completion criteria
- The world clearly owns bounded tournament and intuitive ICM thinking rather than generic chip-EV advice.
- Learners can explain why a tournament decision can differ from a cash-style chip-EV decision in plain language.
- Learners can recognize risk premium, bubble pressure, covering versus covered pressure, and simple tournament-aware closure spots without exact solver output.
- World 8 remains bounded:
  no dense payout math,
  no solver-grade ICM trees,
  and no specialization-owned final-table policy detail.
