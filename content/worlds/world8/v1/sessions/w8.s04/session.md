# Session w8.s04

## Objective
Practice reading bubble pressure before defaulting to ordinary chip-EV aggression.

## Scenario
Each rep shows a simple near-bubble tournament spot.
The learner decides whether payout pressure should make the table more cautious or create extra pressure opportunities against others.

## Decision
Choose whether bubble pressure is the dominant force in the spot and which side it presses harder.

## Explanation
Bubble pressure is one of the clearest first tournament distortions of normal chip play.
The goal is not exact math, only a bounded read on when survival concerns should tighten decisions and when that caution can be pressured by others.
