# Session w1.s10

## Objective
Run the final World 1 checkpoint by combining position awareness, initiative awareness, and simple preflop action quality.

## Scenario
Each rep revisits a compact preflop spot and asks the learner to combine the core World 1 ideas:
where the seat sits,
who is applying pressure,
and whether the starting hand is clean enough to continue.
The goal is to read the role in the hand before clicking an action.
Some hands will still look playable at first glance,
especially from a comfortable seat,
but the checkpoint should catch when pressure turns that continue into trouble.

## Decision
Choose the simplest preflop action that best fits position,
initiative,
and clean start quality in the spot.

## Explanation
This checkpoint closes World 1 by testing whether the learner can keep the early habits together.
The goal is not advanced strategy,
only a stable learner-facing habit of checking seat,
pressure,
and hand quality before choosing the next preflop action.
The final check is whether the learner can release a tempting continue even when the seat looks friendly.
If the hand is weak and pressure is already asking too much from it,
the honest World 1 answer is to let it go before the mistake gets bigger.
