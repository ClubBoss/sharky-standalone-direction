# Session w1.s09

## Objective
Blend first-in and facing-action reads as the final focused World 1 tail block.

## Scenario
Each rep changes whether the learner is first into the pot or reacting to existing pressure.
The goal is to keep the response clean by noticing whether the action is still unopened or has already reached the learner,
and by feeling how seat comfort changes which continue is still honest.
Some continues can look comfortable from a good seat,
but once pressure is already on the hand they still lead into trouble too often to stay clean.

## Decision
Choose the action that best fits the first-in versus facing-action cue in the spot.

## Explanation
This session closes the focused tail before the final World 1 checkpoint.
The learner should still separate clean opens, playable continues, and disciplined folds by reading whether pressure is already on the hand.
Late-position comfort can make a continue easier to justify,
while earlier seats and existing pressure should tighten the response faster.
The bridge habit here is simple:
do not let a comfortable seat talk you into continuing with a hand that only looks playable.
If pressure is already on the hand and the continue starts to feel thin,
World 1 should prefer releasing before that spot turns into avoidable trouble.
