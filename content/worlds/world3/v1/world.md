# World 3

## Overview
- World 3 is the preflop framework bridge.
- This world gives structure without charts: simple hand categories, one clear reason for the first preflop choice, and compact open / call / fold logic that a learner can explain in plain language.
- The goal is not advanced policy or memorized ranges. The goal is to stop random preflop guessing by giving hands a small number of stable buckets and matching those buckets to the simplest useful first action.
- This world belongs after early action-choice stability and before deeper sizing, range-construction, board-texture, or specialization-owned adjustments.

## Goals
- Teach hand categories in a form that is easy to use:
  premium, strong, medium, and trash.
- Connect those categories to the first compact preflop decisions:
  open, call, or fold.
- Keep the logic one reason at a time instead of asking for multi-factor optimization.
- Build confidence that the same hand can deserve a different first action once position and table context are acknowledged, without turning the lesson into chart memorization.
- Prepare the staircase for later worlds where bet purpose, board texture, initiative, and deeper framework layers become more important.

## Completion criteria
- The world clearly owns the preflop framework bridge rather than a later-street competency family.
- Users can sort hands into the first stable categories without needing charts.
- Users can justify a simple open / call / fold choice with one compact reason tied to category and basic context.
- World 3 remains bounded:
  no full preflop charts,
  no advanced range-construction language,
  no specialization-owned cash / tournament deviations.
