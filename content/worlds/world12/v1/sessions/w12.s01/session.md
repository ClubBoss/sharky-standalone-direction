# Session w12.s01

## Objective
Use one repeatable process line to review results, reset after pressure, and
choose the next hand by the current table signals instead of the last outcome.

## Scenario
You are finishing the foundation route with a short pattern replay session.
Across six reps, the job is to protect the same decision loop when results,
emotion, confidence, or pressure try to steer the next action.

### Scenario rep 1

Hero folded a thin river bluff-catcher after a tight line and then saw the
opponent show a bluff. The focus is to review the process before rewriting the
decision based on one reveal.

### Scenario rep 2

Hero made a loose call, won the pot, and feels rewarded by the result. The
focus is to mark the weak process even when the outcome looks good.

### Scenario rep 3

Hero lost a large pot after getting money in ahead and feels anger rising. The
focus is to run a short reset before the next meaningful decision.

### Scenario rep 4

Hero made an avoidable mistake last orbit and now faces a normal hand with a
clear price. The focus is to return to the current table read instead of
chasing correction.

### Scenario rep 5

An opponent needles Hero into wanting a louder line. The focus is to keep the
decision tied to evidence, not ego.

### Scenario rep 6

Hero reaches a final recap spot after several noisy hands. The focus is to name
the process cue that should travel into the next session.

## Decision
For each rep, pick the process-preserving choice. The correct choice may be a
review, a reset, a calm continue, or a disciplined pass, but the reason is the
same: use the current signal before reacting to the last result.

## Explanation
Mindset bridge content is practical only when it changes the next decision. The
source packet keeps the loop concrete: result noise appears, a process cue is
named, the next action follows the current table signal, and review stays short
enough to repeat.
