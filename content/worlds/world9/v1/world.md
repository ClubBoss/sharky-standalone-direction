# World 9

## Overview
- World 9 is the learner-facing home for bounded exploit thinking.
- This world teaches that real opponents do not always follow balanced or ideal lines:
  some overfold,
  some call too often,
  some bluff too much,
  and some become predictable in specific positions or board types.
- The goal is not solver-grade counter-strategy or endless population-node detail. The goal is to help learners recognize common human tendencies, adjust in practical ways, and stay aware that every exploit creates a counter-exploit risk.
- This world comes after board reading, range thinking, stack-depth reasoning, and tournament-pressure basics. Once learners understand what a solid default looks like, the next step is knowing when a real player pool gives permission to deviate.

## Goals
- Teach exploit baseline thinking in plain language rather than dense theoretical jargon.
- Show how to punish common overfolding and how to adjust against calling-station behavior without turning every spot into pure guesswork.
- Introduce frequency control, position-specific exploits, and board-driven exploit filters at a bounded learner-facing level.
- Show how blockers, traps, and counter-exploit awareness affect whether an exploit remains practical.
- Prepare learners for later specialization and sharper population work without turning this world into a solver-heavy or pro-level exploit lab.

## Completion criteria
- The world clearly owns bounded exploit thinking rather than generic action advice.
- Learners can explain why a real-player adjustment can differ from a balanced default in plain language.
- Learners can recognize common overfold, overcall, aggression-frequency, and trap/counter-exploit spots without needing solver output.
- World 9 remains bounded:
  no dense node-lock trees,
  no solver-mimic exploit precision,
  and no specialization-owned pool-specific policy detail.
