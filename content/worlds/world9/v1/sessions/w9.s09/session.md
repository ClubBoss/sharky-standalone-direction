# Session w9.s09

## Objective
Practice late-street exploit closure when a player tendency still affects the final decision.

## Scenario
Each rep shows a late-street spot where earlier reads still matter, but the final action must be chosen with more discipline because the hand is near closure.
The learner decides whether the exploit remains strong enough to carry through on the last street.

## Decision
Choose the late-street closure that best fits the player tendency without forcing an exploit that has gone too thin.

## Explanation
Late-street exploit closure is where overconfidence can be most expensive.
The goal is to recognize when a player read still justifies the adjustment and when the last street demands more restraint than earlier pressure spots.
