# Session w9.s08

## Objective
Practice trap and counter-exploit awareness before overusing a profitable-looking adjustment.

## Scenario
Each rep shows an apparent exploit opportunity but also a sign that the opponent may be trapping or adjusting back.
The learner decides when the exploit should proceed and when caution is needed because the simple read may already be stale.

## Decision
Choose whether to trust the exploit signal or reduce confidence because trap or counter-exploit risk is rising.

## Explanation
Strong exploit play requires knowing when not to over-believe the first read.
Trap and counter-exploit awareness protect the learner from turning a once-good adjustment into an automatic mistake.
