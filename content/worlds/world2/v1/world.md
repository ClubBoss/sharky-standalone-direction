# World 2

## Overview
Cognitive shift: move from "I know the hand ranking" to "I can explain who is ahead, who acts later, and who keeps pressure."
World 2 starts with practical table-reading foundations, then carries those same reads into board texture, improvement counting, price-sensitive continue decisions, and short linked hand chains.

## Goals
- Practice simple showdown comparisons from visible cards and board truth.
- Practice position thinking from seat relationships and action order.
- Practice initiative thinking from who made the last aggressive action.
- Practice simple board-texture reads, outs counting, and price-sensitive continue decisions on the same surfaced table scenes.
- Connect those ideas through short linked chains so World 2 closes as one learner-facing table-reading arc rather than isolated drills.

## Completion criteria
- You can identify a winner at showdown from visible hand and board truth.
- You can identify who is in position from seat relationships.
- You can identify who keeps initiative after the last aggressive action.
- You can read a dynamic board, count a simple improvement path, and decide when the price is good enough to continue or too poor to justify staying in.
- Common mistake we fix: guessing from labels or hope instead of reading the actual table state.
