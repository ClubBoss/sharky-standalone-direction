# Session w2.s14

## Objective
Use one tougher draw-and-price spot to connect board pressure, limited improvement, and disciplined folding.

## Scenario
The same authored flop stays in view while the learner first reads pressure, then counts a weaker improvement path, then decides that the price is too poor to continue.

## Decision
This is still intuition, not formula work. Read the board, count the clean outs, then notice when the draw is not strong enough for the price being offered.

## Explanation
This bridge complements the earlier continue intuition by teaching the other half of the skill: some dynamic boards still require folding when the draw is weaker and the cost is worse.
