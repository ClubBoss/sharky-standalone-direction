# World 5

## Overview
- World 5 shifts attention from your private hand to the board itself.
- This world is the first honest home for board awareness, draw recognition, outs, and improvement counting in a compact learner-facing form.
- The goal is not advanced equity math or pot-odds policy. The goal is to help learners see what the board is doing: which textures are dry or wet, which cards improve draws, and how improvement changes the next reasonable action.
- This world comes after World 4 because purpose and price need to exist first. Once a learner understands what a bet is trying to accomplish, the next step is reading how the board changes that plan.

## Goals
- Teach board texture as a visible pattern-recognition skill:
  dry, wet, paired, connected, and shifted boards.
- Introduce draws and simple improvement counting in concrete terms rather than abstract equations.
- Help learners notice when the board changes relative hand strength and when new cards create or remove pressure.
- Keep action choices tied to board-state reading:
  texture first, then the simplest fitting response.
- Prepare the staircase for later equity, pot-odds, and deeper framework work without pretending those later layers are already mastered here.

## Completion criteria
- The world clearly owns board awareness rather than private-hand-first reasoning alone.
- Users can identify the first stable board-texture buckets and connect them to simple action meaning.
- Users can recognize common draws and count straightforward improvement paths in plain language.
- Users can explain that outs and improvement counting are foundation tools that come before deeper equity or pot-odds judgment.
- World 5 remains bounded:
  no heavy math,
  no advanced solver-style equity policy,
  no specialization-owned cash / tournament adjustments.
