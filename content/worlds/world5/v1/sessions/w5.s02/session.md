# Session w5.s02

## Objective
Strengthen board-state reading by recognizing dry-board patterns and noticing how limited draw or improvement pressure changes the next reasonable response.

## Scenario
You stay in compact board-reading spots. Each rep asks you to look at the board first, recognize the dry texture in plain language, and connect that texture to the most important improvement or pressure cue before settling on any action.

## Decision
Read the board state first: identify the dry texture, then notice what it does and does not allow in terms of draws, improvement, and immediate pressure.

## Explanation
Dry-board discipline starts with reading the board correctly, not with anchor-first action flow. World 5 is still building pattern recognition and simple improvement awareness before deeper equity, pot-odds, and specialization layers appear.
