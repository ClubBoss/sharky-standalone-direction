## Objective
Practice draw-completion reading by noticing how the current board state reveals completed draws, missed improvements, and remaining pressure cues.

## Scenario
You stay in compact board-reading spots. Each rep asks you to read how the board has developed across streets, identify the most important completed draw, missed draw, or improvement cue on the current board, then let that read guide the simplest fitting response.

## Decision
Read the board state first: notice what the texture completed, what it left unfinished, and which improvement cues still matter before choosing the simplest fitting response.

## Explanation
Draw completion matters because the board can confirm pressure, remove bluff paths, or leave missed improvements behind. World 5 still keeps texture, draws, and improvement cues primary, with action treated as downstream of reading the board correctly before deeper equity, pot-odds, or specialization layers appear.
