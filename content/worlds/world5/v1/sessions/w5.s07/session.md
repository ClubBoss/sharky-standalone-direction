# Session w5.s07

## Objective
Practice out-of-position board reading by noticing how texture, draw pressure, and improvement cues shape the next reasonable response.

## Scenario
You stay in compact board-reading spots while out of position. Each rep asks you to identify the most important texture, draw, or improvement cue on the current board first, then use that read to guide the simplest fitting response without treating position alone as the answer.

## Decision
Read the board state first: notice what the texture allows, what draws are live, and what improvement cues matter before choosing the simplest out-of-position response.

## Explanation
Out-of-position play still starts with reading the board correctly. World 5 keeps texture, draws, and improvement cues primary, with action treated as downstream of that board-state read before deeper equity, pot-odds, or specialization layers appear.
