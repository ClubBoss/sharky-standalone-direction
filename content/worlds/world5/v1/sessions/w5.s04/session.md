# Session w5.s04

## Objective
Practice reading turn texture shifts by noticing how the board changes from its earlier state and what new draw or improvement cues become important.

## Scenario
You stay in compact board-reading spots. Each rep asks you to compare the earlier board texture with the shifted turn texture, then identify the most important new pressure, draw, or improvement signal before any action becomes relevant.

## Decision
Read the board state first: notice how the turn changes the texture, then identify which draw paths, blockers, or improvement cues now matter more than before.

## Explanation
Turn shifts matter because the board can change what hands improve and what pressure becomes credible. World 5 still focuses on board-state-led reading, with action treated as downstream of reading texture changes correctly before deeper equity, pot-odds, or specialization layers appear.
