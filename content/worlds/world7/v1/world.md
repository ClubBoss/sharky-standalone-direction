# World 7

## Overview
- World 7 is the learner-facing home for bounded stack-depth thinking.
- This world teaches that the same hand, board, and line do not mean the same thing at every depth:
  shallow stacks reduce room,
  medium stacks create flexible pressure,
  and deep stacks reopen leverage and future-street risk.
- The goal is not solver-grade push/fold or exact tree memorization. The goal is to help learners see how stack depth changes which plans are realistic, which actions create pressure, and which hands can continue comfortably.
- This world comes after board reading and range-thinking basics. Once learners can read boards and think in hand groups, the next step is understanding how stack size changes the value of those reads.

## Goals
- Teach stack-depth buckets in plain language rather than dense technical jargon.
- Show the practical difference between shallow, medium, and deep stacks, including how depth changes maneuverability and commitment pressure.
- Introduce how stack depth changes in-position and out-of-position play without turning the world into a specialization-heavy policy layer.
- Show how blocker effects and late-street closure feel different when stacks are short versus deep.
- Prepare learners for later worlds where tournament pressure and exploit logic build on depth-aware reasoning.

## Completion criteria
- The world clearly owns bounded stack-depth thinking rather than generic action advice.
- Learners can explain why the same spot changes when the effective stack changes.
- Learners can recognize shallow-stack discipline, medium-stack flexibility, and deep-stack leverage at a practical learner-facing level.
- World 7 remains bounded:
  no solver-heavy jam trees,
  no full push/fold chart memorization,
  and no specialization-owned format policy.
