# Session w7.s10

## Objective
Run the final World 7 checkpoint by combining the earlier stack-depth themes into one bounded synthesis read.

## Scenario
Each rep revisits a compact stack-depth spot and asks the learner to combine the major World 7 ideas:
depth bucket,
shallow versus medium versus deep pressure,
position,
and late-street closure risk.
The goal is to read how stack depth changes the whole plan before narrowing to a simple action.

## Decision
Choose the plan that best fits the full stack-depth picture in the spot,
not just one isolated signal.

## Explanation
This checkpoint closes World 7 by testing whether the learner can keep depth logic together as one bounded read.
The goal is not exact push-fold precision or solver-style trees,
only a stable learner-facing habit of combining depth,
position,
and future-street pressure before deciding how the spot should be played.
