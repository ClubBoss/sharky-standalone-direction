# Session w7.s06

## Objective
Teach how stack depth changes in-position planning without turning the spot into generic action advice.

## Scenario
Each rep shows an in-position spot with a specific effective stack and asks which plan still makes practical sense at that depth.
The goal is to notice that position stays powerful,
but shallow stacks narrow your options while deeper stacks let you keep more pressure and control.

## Decision
Choose the in-position plan that best fits the stack depth in the spot.

## Explanation
In position, depth changes how much room you have to apply pressure, delay commitment, or keep weaker hands in.
This session keeps the lesson bounded:
the learner is not solving full trees,
only recognizing how position works differently when the stack is short,
medium,
or deeper.
