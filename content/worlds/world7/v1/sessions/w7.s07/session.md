# Session w7.s07

## Objective
Teach how stack depth changes out-of-position planning in plain learner-facing terms.

## Scenario
Each rep shows an out-of-position spot with a specific effective stack and asks which response is most realistic at that depth.
The goal is to feel that playing first becomes harder when depth changes how much pressure you must absorb and how much room you have to recover.

## Decision
Choose the out-of-position adjustment that best fits the stack depth in the spot.

## Explanation
Out of position, depth changes how safely you can continue and how costly mistakes become.
Shorter stacks reduce room to maneuver,
while deeper stacks increase the burden of acting first across later streets.
This session teaches depth-aware adjustment without widening into later leverage or transition themes.
