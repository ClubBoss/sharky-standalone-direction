# Session w4.s10

## Objective
Run the final World 4 checkpoint by closing denial intent through mixed purpose-and-price reads.

## Scenario
Each rep revisits a compact betting spot and asks whether denial is still the clearest purpose or whether value,
protection,
or bluff pressure better explains the line.
The learner compares how the chosen size changes what the opponent is offered and whether denial still deserves to drive the final preset choice.

## Decision
Choose whether denial is the best practical purpose in the spot,
then choose the size that best matches that purpose and its price effect.

## Explanation
This checkpoint closes World 4 by testing whether purpose categories still stay separate when the cues are mixed.
The goal is not exact sizing optimization,
only a stable learner-facing habit of checking whether the line should lightly tax realization,
make the continue clearly uncomfortable,
or make it expensive immediately before choosing the preset.
