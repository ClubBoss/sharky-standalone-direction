# Session w4.s02

## Objective
Teach bluff and denial as pressure-based sizing purposes in plain learner-facing terms.

## Scenario
Each rep presents one clear pressure job:
push fold pressure now,
deny a cheap continue,
apply a smaller bluff probe,
or reopen pressure without overcommitting.
The learner succeeds by matching the size to the pressure job instead of treating every aggressive size the same.

## Decision
Choose the size that best matches the pressure purpose in the spot.

## Explanation
Bluff and denial are not identical.
A bluff tries to make better hands or unready ranges leave,
while denial often punishes hands that would like a cheap chance to improve.
This session keeps the ideas concrete by tying each purpose to a compact preset and the price it offers.
