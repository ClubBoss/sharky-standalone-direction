# World 4

## Overview
- World 4 is the first honest mainline home for bet purpose and basic price awareness.
- This world teaches that sizing is not decoration. A size expresses what you are trying to accomplish:
  value, protection, denial, bluff pressure, or controlled pot growth.
- The first pass stays compact on purpose. Users should learn what a size is doing before they are asked to optimize close size variants or solve deeper format-specific trees.
- Price matters here in a practical way: half-pot, one-third pot, pot, and smaller legal raises change what opponents are offered and how much pressure the line creates.
- This world sits after early action-choice stability and before deeper preflop framework, advanced board/range work, or specialization-specific policy.

## Goals
- Teach bet purpose before sizing sophistication.
- Connect common size presets to clear intents:
  half-pot for value without pushing too many weaker hands out,
  one-third pot for easier price-keeping,
  pot for maximum immediate pressure,
  minimum raise for the smallest legal reopen when control matters.
- Keep price awareness concrete and visual instead of abstract or math-heavy.
- Reinforce anchor-first flow: confirm the seat, street, and board context before choosing the action or size.
- Establish a clean bridge from simple action choice into later worlds where board texture, draws, stack depth, and specialization can change which size or plan becomes best.

## Completion criteria
- The world presents bet purpose as a mainline competency rather than a side pilot.
- Users can explain the practical link between purpose and price:
  a size changes what worse hands can continue with, how expensive a call becomes, and how much pressure the line applies.
- Users can separate at least the first stable intent families already present in the live slice:
  value, protection, bluff, denial, and controlled reopen.
- World 4 remains bounded:
  no deep sizing optimization,
  no solver-style range splitting,
  no specialization-owned cash / tournament policy.
