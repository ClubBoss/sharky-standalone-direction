# World 10

## Overview
- Goal: post-core specialization through track choice, applying one consistent adjustment in cash, tournament, or mixed play.
- Cognitive shift: stop treating each cue in isolation and learn to justify one integrated track read across seat, board, hole cards, and action.

## Goals
- Choose one clear track baseline before acting.
- Keep seat, board, and hole-card reads tied to the same track story.
- Explain why the final action belongs to the selected track instead of defaulting to habit.
- Finish the core ladder ready to hand off into the chosen track root session without reteaching the World10 bridge.

## Completion criteria
- The learner can name the chosen track and the adjustment it is supposed to reinforce.
- The learner can read seat, board, hole cards, and action as one connected track story across the full core ladder.
- The learner can finish `w10.s10` and enter `cash.s01`, `tournament.s01`, or `mixed.s01` with the correct handoff expectations.

## Release-Grade Finish Proof
- The learner can explain one coherent track read across seat, board, hole cards, and action instead of giving four disconnected reasons.
- The core World10 ladder now ends with a clean synthesis checkpoint and a direct track handoff, so the capstone can close before the followup branch begins.

## Handoff To Track Play
- After `w10.s10`, the learner should already understand whether the next repetition is cash, tournament, or mixed and what adjustment that branch is supposed to reinforce.
- The track roots should deepen the selected plan, not reteach the baseline World10 integration lesson.
