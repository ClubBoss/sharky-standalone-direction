# Poker Rules & Setup

What it is
----------
Poker is a game of strict procedures. Before strategy matters, you must follow the mechanical rules of the table to avoid penalties and forced errors.

Why it matters
----------
If you don't know the rules, you leak money. A "string bet" can force you to call when you meant to raise. Misunderstanding "showdown order" gives free information to opponents.

Rules of thumb
----------
* **One Motion:** Always move chips in a single motion or declare the amount verbally *before* acting.
* **Showdown:** The last aggressor shows first. If checked down, the player left of the button shows first.
* **Min-Raise:** Must be at least the size of the previous raise.
* **Reopening:** An all-in less than a full raise does *not* reopen the betting for you.

Mini example
----------
**Situation:** You are on the **BTN**. MP opens to 10. You want to raise to 30.
**Mistake:** Putting in 10, pausing, then adding 20. Dealer rules it a CALL.
**Correct:** Say "Raise" first, then move chips.

Common mistakes
----------
* **String Betting:** Moving chips in steps.
* **Mucking Winner:** Folding at showdown without seeing the opponent's hand.
* **Acting Out of Turn:** Giving away info before it's your move.

Contrast line
----------
**Amateur:** Blames the dealer for "strict rules."
**Pro:** Uses rules to protect their action and information.
