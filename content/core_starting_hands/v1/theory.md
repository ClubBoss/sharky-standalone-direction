# Starting Hands

## Key Idea
- Starting hand quality and table position work together.
- Early positions should play tighter because many players are still behind.
- Late positions can open wider because they act later and control more decisions.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens and faces many players behind, so weak offsuit hands are usually folds.
- Button opens with fewer players left to act and can include more suited and connected hands.
- Same hand can be a fold early and a playable open late.

## Working Rules
- Prioritize strong pairs, big broadway hands, and suited aces in early seats.
- Add more suited connectors and weaker broadways as position improves.
- Avoid dominated offsuit hands from early and middle positions.
- Short stacks require tighter opens and cleaner all-in decisions.
- Loose tables reward value-heavy starts more than speculative starts.
