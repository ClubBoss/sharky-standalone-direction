# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- [[term:BANKROLL]] rules, shot-taking protocols, stop-loss caps, and game selection keep [[term:VARIANCE]] from breaking you, so move up/down only when conditions match your roll and resilience.

## Mini-Example
- With a 50 buy-in NL25 roll, a losing streak hits -3 buy-ins; you stop after your cap, log hands, and drop to NL16 until the roll rebuilds.

## Actionable Rules
- Cash games: keep 40-50 full buy-ins online, 20-30 live, and add 10-20 for short-handed or high-variance lines.
- Tournaments: keep 100-200 buy-ins, rising to 200-300+ for turbos or huge fields with top-heavy payouts.
- Take shots only with 5-10 extra buy-ins over the minimum, and end the shot after a 2-3 buy-in loss.
- Cap any session at 2-4 buy-ins lost to protect decision quality, then review notes offline.
- Choose softer games and favorable rake, tracking win rate by table type to inform game selection.

## Quick Check
- When is it time to move down, and how does your roll size guide that choice?
- What are the key stop-loss settings that keep your play sharp?
