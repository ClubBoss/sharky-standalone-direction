# What to do
Assign each combo group to a weighting category (value, bluff, protection, merge) based on its strength and leverage.

# Input
Groups: AA/KK/QQ, AK, AQs/KQs, JT/T9, 98/87, 76, 55/44, 33/22, ATo, KJo.

# Expected reasoning
Value density sits with the top pairs and suited broadways, so they get highest weight. Bluff candidates come from low-equity, non-blocking combos like ATo and KJo when fold equity exists. Protection weight should cover fragile medium pairs and connectors (55/44/33/22) that must call or check. Merge weight fits the mid connectors (JT/T9/98) that can shift roles. Every combination gets a frequency consistent with its ability to realize equity or carry pressure.

# Correct answer
Value: AA/KK/QQ, AK, AQs/KQs; Bluff: ATo, KJo; Protection: 55/44/33/22; Merge: JT/T9/98/87/76.

# Common mistakes
- Flattening everything into a single weight and losing structure.
- Overweighting marginal bluffs like AK or AQ.
- Ignoring the need for protection weight on small pairs.
- Using merge weight for all mid combos instead of splitting.
- Treating connectors as bluffs without considering leverage.
