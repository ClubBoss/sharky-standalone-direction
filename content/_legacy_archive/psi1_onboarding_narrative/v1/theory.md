### Psi-1 Onboarding Narrative

Poker Analyzer greets new users with a guided path that links visuals, persona logic, and coaching. You start with clear context: what the app does, how V4 visual runtime supports clarity, and how persona signals lead to tailored advice. The path is concise and professional: Sharky, the guide, speaks plainly as an expert analyst, not a cartoon. Each step shows what to expect and what you control, building confidence before your first interactive spot.

[[IMAGE: psi1_map | onboarding map]]

Stage 1: Orientation. You learn the layout, including table visuals powered by the V4 runtime: readable colors, consistent spacing, and clear surface states. You see the toolbar, action buttons, bet sizing lane, hole cards, and table surface. Sharky explains that all labels are ASCII-only and that tooltips come from the inline explanation binder. A quick note confirms that V4 visuals can be toggled but default on for onboarding so you see the final theme.

Stage 2: Persona introduction. The app runs a Tier-A emotion pass to read passive signals, then the Emotional State Machine (ESM) refines states like momentum or struggle. Attention/Tone Model (ATM) tracks focus and communication tone. These feed the behavioral fusion, dynamics, and persona-driven signals. Sharky explains that this stack keeps advice consistent: the coaching layer uses fused signals to decide pacing, risk, and focus. You can view summaries, but onboarding stays lightweight.

Stage 3: Coaching overview. Persona advice produces actionable hints; explanations clarify why; heuristics and coaching hooks turn them into tips. Multi-stage recommendations outline immediate, mid, and long suggestions. Surface integration turns them into tooltips and inline help. Consistency checks run in the background; if cohesion or persona data misalign, the system flags it before showing advice.

Stage 4: First interactive spot primer. You will enter a simple SRP BTN vs BB spot in Psi-2. The goal is to practice a small cbet on a dry flop, see how coaching responds, and observe V4 visuals during action. Sharky cues you: watch the action buttons, check the help icon, and note how persona hints match the spot. Tier-A and ESM update in real time; coaching reacts without distracting animations.

Stage 5: Safe experimentation. The onboarding encourages quick cycles: act, read a brief tip, adjust. No long forms, no pop-up overload. If V4 is off, visuals fall back to V3 without changing coaching. Data stays ASCII-only; all maps are deterministic. You can replay the sequence to feel how the system stabilizes under repeat decisions.

Stage 6: Summary and next steps. Sharky recaps the flow: V4 visuals for clarity, persona engines for state and tone, coaching for pacing and action, and a checked pipeline for cohesion. You are ready to move into Psi-2 with a clear, consistent experience. The onboarding emphasizes control, transparency, and minimal friction so you can focus on decisions, not the interface.
