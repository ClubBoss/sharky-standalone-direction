

### Source structure
Each module resides in `content/<module_name>/v1/` with:
- `theory.md` - narrative and rules of thumb
- `demos.jsonl` - demo step flows (SpotKind-tagged)
- `drills.jsonl` - single-token drills

What it is
The program catalog is a structured index of all training modules organized by format (Core, Cash, MTT, HU, Math, Online, Live). Each module targets one topic using fixed action tokens (3bet_ip_9bb, 3bet_oop_12bb, 4bet_ip_21bb, 4bet_oop_24bb, small_cbet_33, half_pot_50, big_bet_75, size_down_dry, size_up_wet, protect_check_range, delay_turn, probe_turns, double_barrel_good, triple_barrel_scare, call, fold, overfold_exploit). Core modules (1-15) establish fundamentals; format tracks (Cash 16-30, MTT 31-43, HU 44-50, Math 51-58, Online 59-70, Live 71-81) apply those fundamentals to specific game types.

Why it matters
The catalog maps your learning path and ensures coverage across all poker domains. Clear numbering and categories help you find relevant modules quickly, track progress, and identify gaps in your knowledge. Understanding the catalog structure prevents wasting time on redundant material and helps you prioritize based on your game format and skill level.

Rules of thumb
- Start with Core modules 1-15 for baseline strategy covering position concepts, pot odds and equity math, starting hand selection, and fundamental bet sizing families before diving into any format-specific tracks.
- Cash players focus on modules 16-30 after completing Core foundation; prioritize single raised pots, 3-bet pots, and blind defense first, then add squeeze strategy and multiway adjustments.
- MTT players cover modules 31-43 with emphasis on ICM basics, short/mid/deep stack play strategies, and bubble dynamics including PKO bounty considerations.
- HU specialists work through modules 44-50 for heads-up specific adjustments to preflop opening ranges, postflop continuation betting, turn barreling decisions, and river value/bluff thresholds.
- Math modules 51-58 support all formats; cover pot odds, equity calculations, and ICM math early in your development to strengthen real-time decision-making accuracy.
- Online players add modules 59-70 for pool reads, HUD usage patterns, and exploit strategies tailored to fast-fold dynamics and multi-table tournament environments.
- Live players overlay modules 71-81 on top of Cash or MTT foundation for physical tells, proper etiquette, table and position selection, and live-specific procedures like chip handling and floor call protocols.

Mini example
New player path: Start Core 1-15 to learn positions (UTG/MP/CO/BTN/SB/BB in module 2), pot odds (module 3), starting hands (module 4), flop fundamentals (module 5), turn planning (module 6), river play (module 7), and bet sizing families (module 10). Cash player continues to modules 16-30 for blind defense from BB vs BTN opens, CO squeeze spots, and multiway dynamics.

Common mistakes
- Skipping Core modules and jumping directly to format tracks leads to weak fundamentals and inconsistent execution across different positions.
- Studying modules out of sequence causes knowledge gaps; for example, learning 3-bet pots (module 18) before understanding SPR and bet sizing families (modules 9-10).
- Ignoring Math modules (51-58) results in poor intuition for equity calculations, ICM decisions, and EV estimates.

Mini-glossary
Core: Foundation modules covering universal poker concepts applicable to all formats.
Track: Format-specific module group (Cash, MTT, HU, Math, Online, Live).
Module: Single topic with theory, demos, and drills organized under standardized structure.
Token: Fixed action from the approved set used consistently across all modules.

EV: Expected Value - the average amount you'd win or lose if you made the same play many times.
