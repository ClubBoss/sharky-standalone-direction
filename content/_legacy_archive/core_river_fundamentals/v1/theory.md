# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- The river freezes equity and chips, so decide between [[term:POLARIZATION]], [[term:THIN_VALUE]], and bluffcatch based on [[term:BLOCKERS]], sizing, and the showdown path you want.


## Mini-Example
- CO bets flop/turn, BTN calls. River bricks; CO overbets with top set as a polar line, BTN bluffcatches with a medium hand, calls, and loses. Bettor shows first because the river bet was called.

## Actionable Rules
- [[term:POLARIZATION]] on blanks when you own nut advantage: mix bet_big_river or overbets with your best values and bluffs.
- [[term:THIN_VALUE]] in position against wide calling ranges using bet_small_river to snag worse top or second pairs.
- Bluff with [[term:BLOCKERS]] that deny strong calls and do not unblock folds.
- Check back marginal showdown hands, either IP or OOP, when neither side has a clear edge.
- Bluffcatch only when price is fair and villain is polarized with enough missed draws; otherwise check-fold.
- Follow showdown flow: bettor_shows_first after a river bet, and if the river checks through the first active left of the button shows.
