# What to do
Decide whether the snapshot creates mid or high pressure based on top-end density and continuity.

# Input
Range A: uncapped, strong top-end continuity, high card blockers, thin mid density. Range B: capped mid-band, weaker top-end, wide blockers with low aggression.

# Expected reasoning
Range A’s uncapped top-end and continuity suggest high pressure, yet thin mids cap it.
Range B’s capped mid-band lacks continuity and cannot push high tiers.
Mid pressure wins because A forces action while B caps escalation.

# Correct answer
Mid pressure tier: uncapped top-end forces action but capped mid-band keeps the overall pressure from reaching high.

# Common mistakes
- Confusing uncapped top-end with automatic high pressure.
- Overlooking continuity and betting for high tier.
- Misreading capped mids as high aggression zones.
- Flattening the snapshot to low pressure.
- Ignoring how blockers mute density.
