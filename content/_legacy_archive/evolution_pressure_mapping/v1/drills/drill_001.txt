# What to do
Name the pressure tier (low, mid, high) suggested by the range versus range snapshot.

# Input
Range A: top-end continuity, capped mid-band, few loose bluffs. Range B: weaker top-end, denser mid-range, double-barrel aggression.

# Expected reasoning
# Expected reasoning
Range A’s capped mid-band and light aggression keep pressure low despite its continuity.
Range B’s density and aggression reach mid pressure but cannot climb without top-end leverage.
High tier needs aggression and blockers; here the cap and middles keep it low.

# Correct answer
Low pressure tier: Range A’s cap forces cautious lines while Range B’s density never reaches high leverage.

# Common mistakes
- Confusing B’s aggression with high pressure.
- Overweighting Range A’s top-end as a signal to blast.
- Flattening the mid-tier and calling it mid pressure.
- Ignoring the capping effect on Range A’s node.
- Assuming top-end strength always creates pressure.
