# Scenario
IP pushes heavier pressure; OOP must read density, continuity, and cap cues to classify the pressure tier.

# Snapshot
Attacker: strong top/mid density, consistent continuity. Defender: fragile mid-band, clear capped segments, barely resisting pressure. Signal: aggression climbing.

# Player task
Decide whether the tier is low, mid, or high pressure and explain using the density/cap indicators.

# Expected reasoning
Thick top/mid density with seamless continuity sustains pressure, pushing the tier upward. Defender caps bend under aggression, so the node is not low. The fragile mid-band folds before rebuilding, so high pressure is achievable because aggression keeps rising. The capped segments keep it from collapsing into mid pressure, backing a high-pressure classification.

# Correct line
High pressure: continuity plus strong density overwhelms caps and drives aggression.

# Why not the alternatives
- Low pressure: ignores the rising aggression from continuity. 
- Mid pressure: understates how the caps fold under sustained pressure. 
- No pressure: misreads the density surge as passive. 
- Polar: assumes aggressive folds instead of real pressure. 
- Timing: confuses window type with pressure tier.
