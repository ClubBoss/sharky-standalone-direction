# Recap

## Key Ideas
- Equity realization depends on position, initiative, SPR, texture, sizing, and player count; controlling those factors turns raw equity into chips.
- Acting last and keeping SPR manageable lets you convert medium-strength hands cleanly while heads-up pots realize more value than multiway.

## Common Mistakes
- Calling OOP with weak holdings that realize poorly, only to fold later and leak chips.
- Ignoring SPR and texture when sizing, arriving at rivers where your stack cannot credibly value bet or bluff.

## Quick Checks
- What combination of factors boosts equity realization the most?
- How does managing SPR protect your river plans?

## Mini-Exercise (1–2 lines)
- Given a dry board with initiative, note how you size to keep SPR low and force folds on later streets.
