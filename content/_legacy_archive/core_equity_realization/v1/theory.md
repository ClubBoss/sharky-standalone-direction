# Theory Micro-Loop


- ## Key Idea
- [[term:EQUITY_REALIZATION]] depends on position, initiative, [[term:SPR]], texture, sizing, and player count; you realize more when you control the pot and keep [[term:SPR]] manageable.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens 2.3 bb, CO calls. Flop K72r: UTG c-bets small, CO calls. Turn 5: UTG bets small again, [[term:DENY_EQUITY]] CO's overcards, and CO folds. UTG realized [[term:EQUITY]] by acting first of range, choosing small sizes, and keeping [[term:SPR]] low for a clean river.

## Actionable Rules
- Use position and initiative to realize more: last aggressor can bet, check, or free card to keep [[term:EQUITY]] alive.
- Keep [[term:SPR]] low with medium-strength hands so top pair/overpairs commit without folding to pressure.
- Match texture and sizing: dry boards reward small c-bets, while wet boards need larger bets to deny equity and protect your share.
- Realize draws better heads-up; avoid multiway bloating because extra players shrink fold equity and showdown chances.
- Plan your turn/river [[term:BET_SIZING]] to reach a desirable [[term:SPR]] (e.g., turn bet 70% to keep eventual [[term:SPR]] under 1) so [[term:BET_SIZING]] and bluffs stay credible.
