# What to do
Classify the range advantage state and explain which exploitation lane becomes viable.

# Input
Attacker keeps stronger top-end combos and cohesive mids while the defender is semi-capped with fragile mid combos and limited aggression.

# Expected reasoning
Density edge sits at the top and mid bands, giving leverage while fragility zones warn against overcommitment. Pressure cues point toward mid/high tiers yet defender reactions stay call-heavy, so layering protects the mid band. Advantage grows slowly as fragility persists, opening thin-value or timing-pressure lanes before the defender yields.

# Correct answer
Controlled advantage: thin-value or timing-pressure lane is viable once the defender yields.

# Common mistakes
- Overreading the density edge as blanket dominance.
- Ignoring fragility before launching bluffs.
- Forcing aggression into the low-pressure node because it looks good.
- Confusing thin-value with stab pressure.
- Treating advantage as static and ignoring reaction shifts.
