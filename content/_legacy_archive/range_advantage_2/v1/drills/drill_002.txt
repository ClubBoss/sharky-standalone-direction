# What to do
Decide whether this scenario signals full advantage, controlled advantage, or only a density edge.

# Input
Attacker shows a stronger top-end but the defender keeps cohesive mid combinations and minimal fragility.

# Expected reasoning
Top-end strength gives leverage, but defender cohesion prevents pressure from running away. Density edge exists, yet fragility zones remain closed, so pressure tier stays mid-level. Reaction layer leans call-heavy with occasional checks, so true advantage cannot manifest. Stability in the mids keeps thin-value lines tentative and timing windows narrow until the defender cracks.

# Correct answer
Density edge only: no full advantage until the defender’s mids decay.

# Common mistakes
- Overvaluing the top-end as full advantage.
- Ignoring defender cohesion and assuming fragility.
- Inflating pressure tier despite the mid-level resistance.
- Misreading the call-heavy reaction layer as a fold shift.
- Mistaking a density edge for a timing-window trigger.
