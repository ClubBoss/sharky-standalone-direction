# What it is
Range Advantage 2.0 is a layered model that merges top-end density, mid-band cohesion, fragility zones, pressure tiers, and reaction alignment so you see leverage and timing in one place.

# Why it matters
This clarity keeps later streets grounded: you avoid over-stabbing, stabilize thin-value bets, and only pursue exploit seeds when density, pressure, and reaction signals all agree.

# Rules of thumb
- Read the top-end first, then layer density to confirm leverage.
- Identify fragility zones before committing aggression.
- Align layering with pressure expectations so protection layers flex appropriately.
- Confirm the reaction layer before expanding value.
- Avoid over-reading tiny density gaps as advantage.
- Advantage grows when reaction shifts favor aggression, not merely because density looks good.
- Thin value needs stable mids to stay credible.
- Keep adjustment loops tight so tweaks reinforce existing signals.

# Mini example
Attacker maintains heavier top-end and cohesive mids while the defender stays semi-capped with fragile middles. Range Advantage 2.0 reads a mid/high-pressure tier, call-heavy reactions, and a fragility zone where a thin-value or timing-pressure lane opens once the defender yields. Aggression stays measured until that window appears.

# Common mistakes
- Equating a density edge with full advantage.
- Ignoring fragility and letting sensitive segments fold.
- Forcing aggression into low-pressure nodes because density looks thick.
- Confusing thin-value with stab pressure and overbetting.
- Missing reaction-layer counter-shifts that demand patience.
- Treating advantage as static instead of watching how it shifts.
- Overweighting minor blockers and neglecting the real backbone.
- Chasing windows when pressure is minimal.

# Workflow
- Isolate the top-end density to confirm carriers.
- Assess cohesion and fragility to understand the mid band.
- Map the pressure tier to see how much aggression the node tolerates.
- Read reaction alignment to determine whether calls, folds, or raises dominate.
- Choose advantage-consistent lines and respect fragility.
- Decide whether thin-value or timing-pressure paths earn the next bet.

# Mini-glossary
- advantage backbone : the combos that supply leverage.
- cohesion : how well mid-band combos stick together.
- fragility zone : segments that collapse under pressure.
- pressure tier : the aggression level that guides sizing.
- reaction alignment : the defender’s dominant mix of responses.
- thin-value lane : a low-key pressure path.
- timing-pressure lane : a conditional lane waiting for the reaction layer to soften.
- density edge : the numerical advantage in combos.
- controlled aggression : measured betting that respects fragility.
- exploit seed : the moment a leak becomes actionable.

# EV
Range Advantage 2.0 boosts EV by syncing aggression with layered signals, protecting thin-value/timing-pressure paths, and reducing misreads in marginal nodes.
