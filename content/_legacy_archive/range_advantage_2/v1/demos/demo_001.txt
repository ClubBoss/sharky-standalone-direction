# Scenario
IP fires a modest continuation bet on a medium texture; OOP must read whether the advantage state justifies aggressive sequencing or if a density edge demands patience.

# Snapshot
Attacker holds a strong top-end and cohesive mid band. Defender is semi-capped, shows only mild fragility in the mids, and the pressure tier sits around mid levels; reaction lines lean toward calls with occasional probing checks.

# Player task
Determine whether this node represents full advantage, controlled advantage, or merely a density edge.

# Expected reasoning
Top-end strength confirms leverage, yet the defender maintains cohesion so fragility zones remain under control. Density edge exists, but the pressure tier never climbs high enough to sweep the defender. Reaction alignment stays semi-stable with call-heavy tendencies, so it will not flip to fold-heavy without more disruption. Layer alignment shows the protection band still intact, limiting how far you can press. Advantage grows incrementally, so thin-value or timing-pressure lanes are the only responsible options before the defender cracks.

# Correct line
Controlled advantage with thin value or timing-pressure option.

# Why not the alternatives
- Overvaluing density as full advantage. 
- Ignoring the defender’s cohesion and assuming fragility. 
- Expecting full pressure when the tier is mid. 
- Mislabeling thin-value pressure as a stab. 
- Treating advantage as static despite call-heavy reactions.
