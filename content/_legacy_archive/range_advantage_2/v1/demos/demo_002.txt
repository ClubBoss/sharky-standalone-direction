# Scenario
OOP checks and IP fires a measured probe; the node demands a determination of whether the advantage has truly materialized.

# Snapshot
Attacker retains strong top-end combos and competent mids, while the defender stays semi-capped with a stable mid-band, mild fragility, and a low to mid pressure tier; reaction lines appear steady.

# Player task
Classify whether the situation shows full advantage, controlled advantage, or only a density edge.

# Expected reasoning
Top-end strength gives a numerical edge, yet the defender’s cohesion denies easy leverage.
Mid cohesion stays intact so fragility zones never open.
The pressure tier hovers in the low/mid range, resisting escalation.
Reaction alignment favors calls and checks rather than folds.
Layering stays protective, so you cannot reliably expand value.
Density edge exists but cannot turn into aggression without a reaction shift.
Thin-value lanes remain tentative until the defender softens.
Timing-pressure windows require clearer weakness than this environment provides.

# Correct line
Density edge only.

# Why not the alternatives
- Overreading the top-end as full advantage.
- Assuming pressure escalates despite the low tier.
- Ignoring defender stability and chasing fragility that is not there.
- Confusing tentative thin-value lanes with legitimate advantage.
- Treating advantage as fixed rather than watching for reaction shifts.
