### Psi-3 Marketing Analytics Recap

Psi-3 shows how Poker Analyzer turns early user actions into analytics that align persona, coaching, and V4 visuals. The funnel stages cover arrival, orientation (Psi-1), first action (Psi-2), Psi-3 depth, and retention checks on day 1, day 3, and day 7. Each stage tracks accuracy, speed, and friction with deterministic logging.

Early signals: accuracy measures correct decisions; speed records time-to-act buckets; friction counts help taps and backtracks. Tier-A reads passive signals; ESM converts them into states like momentum or struggle; AT tracks attention and tone. These feed fusion and coaching, which produce tone and pacing hints. Marketing reads the same signals to time nudges: short sessions for tension users, longer for calm users, and balanced pacing for most.

V4 visuals ensure clarity and consistent logging. Action buttons, bet sizing, hole cards, and surfaces keep fixed spacing and colors. Cohesion gates and token checks prevent inconsistent data from reaching analytics; if visuals or persona data misalign, events pause until resolved. Inline tooltips and help icons come from the explanation binder, so interactions stay predictable.

Session-length heuristics: 10-15 minutes for tension-prone users, 20-30 minutes for calm users, and short repeats for high-focus but variance-sensitive users. Marketing and coaching share these heuristics to avoid overload while encouraging repeat opens. XP and reward weights remain bounded; persona modifiers adjust tone and pacing without altering core gameplay.

First 24h -> 7d conversion: day 0 captures install to first action; day 1 checks repeat opens and completion of the first spot; day 3 monitors new board textures tried; day 7 measures streak continuity and retention. Engagement and friction patterns inform whether to send supportive, balanced, or direct messages. Sharky guidance stays concise, reinforcing the act-read-adjust loop.

Outcome: Psi-3 delivers a single source of truth for onboarding analytics. Visuals, persona signals, coaching outputs, and marketing events align through deterministic checks. You know which signals matter and how they influence pacing, risk, and focus. This prepares you for Psi-4, where more complex scenarios and retention experiments build on the same stable analytics pipeline. The emphasis remains on short, repeatable loops that keep data clean and guidance consistent from day 0 through day 7.
