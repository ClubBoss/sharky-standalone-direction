# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- Flop value comes from range-vs-range thinking. Board categories (dry, wet, paired, monotone, two-tone, monotone, dynamic) guide whether you c-bet small, c-bet big, check back, or plan [[term:PROBE]]s/raises.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens, BTN calls, flop is K72 rainbow. UTG capitalizes on [[term:RANGE_ADVANTAGE]] by c-betting small twice; the static texture keeps BTN on the defensive and a delayed c-bet would only eat [[term:EQUITY_DENIAL]].
- Same players see T98 two-tone. UTG upsizes, the wet board gives BTN real draws, and the bigger bet denies [[term:EQUITY_DENIAL]] while building value ahead of a likely dynamic turn.

## Actionable Rules
- Dry flop + range edge → cbet_small (25-33%) to charge floats and keep pressure while letting [[term:EQUITY_DENIAL]] shifts happen slowly.
- Wet/two-tone textures → cbet_big (60-100%) or selective checks; defend only with real equity or nut [[term:BLOCKERS]] to avoid letting draws run free.
- Monotone/paired → mix check_backs, delay c-bets when turns add equity or unblock folds, and avoid big bets that invite stronger combos.
- Donk_bet/[[term:PROBE]]_turn only when the caller gains nut_advantage (e.g., defending OOP on wet textures) or the aggressor caps by checking back.
- Raise_flop with top value/strong bluffs when textures align; float_call in position versus tiny cbets when turn cards keep options open.
- Remember: EV comes from disciplined sizing, initiative-aware plans, and using size_down_dry/size_up_wet plus small_cbet_33/half_pot_50/big_bet_75 families.
