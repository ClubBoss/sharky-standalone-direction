# Recap

## Key Ideas
- Flop value depends on range-vs-range thinking and board categories; each texture informs whether you c-bet small, c-bet big, check back, or plan probes/raises.
- Dry boards favor compact bets and careful pressure, while wet boards require larger bets or selective checks; monotoe/paired boards steer you toward delayed c-bets or float calls that align with texture shifts.

## Common Mistakes
- Firing big on every flop regardless of texture, which lets draws realize equity or gives better hands inflated prices.
- Calling or bluffing blindly on wet boards without recognizing nut advantage shifts or failing to plan probes/donks.

## Quick Checks
- When do you choose a small c-bet versus a big c-bet?
- What prompts a probe_turn or delayed c-bet after checking the flop?

## Mini-Exercise (1–2 lines)
- Pick a plan for T98tt with a centered range and note when you'd stretch to a big c-bet or check back into a probe turn.
