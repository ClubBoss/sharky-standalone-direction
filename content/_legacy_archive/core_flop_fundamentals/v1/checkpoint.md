# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Range-vs-range thinking and board texture determine whether you c-bet small, c-bet big, check, or plan probes.
- Dry boards allow small bets, while wet/paired textures need larger pressure or selective checks/raises.
- Donk-bets and probe-turns should respond when opponents gain nut advantage or when checking the flop sets up a better turn opportunity.

## Self-Assessment
- I match my flop sizing to texture and know when to check back, delay, or probe.
- I avoid automatic big bets on every board and only ramp pressure when draws and equity align.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- You lead on T98tt with blockers; outline whether you upsize, check back, or plan a probe turn.

## Reflection
- Stay disciplined by letting texture and initiative guide flop plans instead of relying on gut feelings about the board.
