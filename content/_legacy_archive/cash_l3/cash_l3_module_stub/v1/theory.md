## Overview
- Deepen cash-table decision making beyond Level 2 preflop charts.
- Focus on stack-depth aware aggression and fold equity maintenance.
- Emphasize exploit-aware deviations without breaking core ranges.
- Reinforce discipline on marginal hands in multiway pots.
- Build habits for clean value extraction versus loose callers.

## Core Concepts
### Stack Depth Filters
- Adjust open sizes and 3-bet ranges by effective stacks (20bb, 40bb, 80bb).
- Protect calling ranges when short; widen value 3-bets when deep.

### Fold Equity Management
- Size to pressure capped ranges; avoid bloating pots versus sticky stations.
- Prefer lines that keep weaker pairs and draws indifferent to calling.

## Practical Examples
### Example 1: BTN vs BB, 40bb, Single Raised Pot
- Preflop: BTN opens 2.2bb, BB calls.
- Flop K72r: BTN c-bets small, BB calls.
- Turn 5: BTN checks to control; river value bet thin with Kx, check back weak pairs.

### Example 2: CO vs BTN 3-Bet, 80bb, Value Configuration
- Preflop: CO opens 2.5bb, BTN 3-bets 8bb, CO calls with JJ.
- Flop T83r: CO check-calls small; turn low blank, continue check-call.
- River overcard: check-evaluate; fold to large polar bets, call modest sizing.

## Common Mistakes
- Over-bluffing short stacks that are price-insensitive.
- Calling 3-bets out of position with dominated offsuit broadways.
- Triple-barreling stations without range advantage or nut blockers.

## Quick Summary
- Let stack depth guide size, range width, and aggression.
- Preserve fold equity with disciplined sizing; avoid spew versus sticky players.
- Extract thin value when ranges stay wide; tighten when ranges cap and pots grow.
