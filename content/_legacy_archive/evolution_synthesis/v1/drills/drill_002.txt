# What to do
Deliver a synthesis read when the attacker shows uneven top/mid density, defender has partial caps, the tier is high, and reactions tilt fold-heavy.

# Input
Attacker: strong top-end, thin mid density, driving high pressure. Defender: partial caps, weak mid-band, folding more than calling.

# Expected reasoning
Top-end density survives while mids collapse under the high tier.
Caps fold when pressured, so reactions tilt fold-heavy instead of call-heavy.
That reaction opens a stab window if you sequence pressure → reaction correctly.

# Correct answer
High pressure + fold-heavy → stab window: attack the collapsing mid band with controlled stabs before density rebuilds.

# Common mistakes
- Treating caps as stable and waiting for a timing window.
- Separating top-end density from pressure/reaction.
- Flattening reactions into call-heavy due to occasional defenses.
- Calling the window thin-value instead of stab.
- Ignoring that fold-heavy reactions reward stabs.
