# Scenario
Attacker applies asymmetric pressure with more top-end than mid support; defender must integrate range shape, continuity, caps, pressure tier, reaction layer, and window cues to form a read.

# Snapshot
Attacker: high top density with patchy mid continuity. Defender: partial caps, fragile mid band, calling but wary. Pressure tier: mid-high. Reaction: edging toward fold-heavy. Window cue: stretched fold potential before caps harden.

# Player task
Synthesize the cues into one judgment that links structure, pressure, reaction, and window.

# Expected reasoning
High top density with weak mid continuity means pressure leans toward high tiers but lacks support, so defender stays fold-heavy with caps tightening. The pressure tier climbs, and reactions favor folding, which opens a timing-to-fold window rather than a pure stab. The synthesis chain: structure lacks mid continuity, pressure drives fold reactions, cap fragility yields a fold-window opportunity that must be timed before caps rebuild.

# Correct line
High pressure + fold-heavy reaction → fold window: keep pressure honest while tracks caps and triggers folds.

# Why not the alternatives
- Isolating pressure: ignores lack of mid continuity that keeps reactions fold-heavy. 
- Overvaluing blockers: assumes defender will call instead of folding. 
- Treating the window as stab: misreads fold-heavy cues. 
- Merging pressure with timing: misses that fold window, not timing, is available. 
- Neglecting caps: would push toward polar or thin-value incorrectly.
