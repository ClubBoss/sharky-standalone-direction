# Theory Micro-Loop


- ## Key Idea
- Every [[term:BET_SIZING]] has a purpose: connect small/medium bets to [[term:MERGE]] ranges and static textures, and reserve large/overbets for [[term:POLARIZATION]] ranges facing dynamic boards or capped opponents.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens, BTN calls. Flop K72r → small c-bet (~33%) keeps [[term:RANGE_ADVANTAGE]]. Turn 5 adds draws → UTG switches to a medium size (~60%) to charge while keeping value. River bricks → pole size shifts to overbet with strong Kx and bluffs, while medium strength checks.

## Actionable Rules
- Use small bets on static textures with [[term:RANGE_ADVANTAGE]]; they keep the pot manageable while charging marginal hands.
- Use medium bets when you want two or more streets on mixed textures and the pot can grow without forcing folds.
- Use large bets (66-100%) on dynamic textures or with high [[term:EQUITY]] hands and bluffs that deserve fold [[term:EQUITY]].
- Use overbets (125-200%) when your [[term:POLARIZATION]] range is capped and villain must fold many better hands.
- Pair [[term:BET_SIZING]] with range shape: [[term:POLARIZATION]] uses large/overbet, [[term:MERGE]] uses small/medium; mismatch leaks value and confuses opponents.
- Keep [[term:FREQUENCY]] coherent so similar spots use similar lines and sizing families (size_down_dry, size_up_wet, small_cbet_33, half_pot_50, big_bet_75).
