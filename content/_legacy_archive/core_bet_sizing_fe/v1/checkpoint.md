# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Match sizes to texture and range shape: small/medium for merge, large/overbet for polar.
- Keep frequency coherent with size families like size_down_dry, size_up_wet, small_cbet_33, half_pot_50, big_bet_75.
- Use overbets when your polar range meets a capped opponent; shrink to medium when you seek multiple streets.

## Self-Assessment
- I explain why certain textures call for small versus large bets.
- I keep polar and merge ranges distinct through consistent sizing choices.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- You're on a dry board heading to the turn with a merged range; describe how you size now and next.

## Reflection
- Treat sizing as a tool: let texture and range drive size choices instead of defaulting to a favorite number.
