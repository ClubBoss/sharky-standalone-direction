# What to do
Identify the dominant reaction layer (fold-heavy, call-heavy, or raise-heavy) suggested by the snapshot.

# Input
Range A applies moderate pressure with top-end continuity and blocker probes. Range B shows mixed density: capped tight pairs, loose middles that tend to call through pressure.

# Expected reasoning
A’s pressure compresses B’s capped zone, nudging it toward defensive reactions.
B’s loose middles still prefer calling, so the stabilizer layer leans call-heavy.
Raise-heavy only works if blockers back aggression; here the capped zone resists full raises.

# Correct answer
Call-heavy reaction layer: pressure keeps the capped area flat while the middle density calls through, resisting raises.

# Common mistakes
- Labeling the capped zone as raise-heavy.
- Flattening the mixed density into a purely fold-heavy reaction.
- Forcing raises because of A’s pressure alone.
- Ignoring how B’s middles stabilize via calls.
- Equating continuity with unchecked aggression.
