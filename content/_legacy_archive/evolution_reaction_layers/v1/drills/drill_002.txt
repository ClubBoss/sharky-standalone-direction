# What to do
Identify whether this snapshot triggers a fold-heavy, call-heavy, or raise-heavy reaction layer.

# Input
Attacker: strong top-end continuity and steady pressure. Defender: partial caps, thin mid-density, occasional blocker calls.

# Expected reasoning
Top-end continuity keeps aggression on, forcing defensive reactions.
Partial caps and thin density cannot sustain raises, so the defender leans toward folding.
Blocker calls appear but without density they cannot support a call-heavy layer.

# Correct answer
Fold-heavy reaction layer: the defender’s caps and density collapse under steady pressure.

# Common mistakes
- Treating mixed caps as raise-heavy openings.
- Ignoring thin density when deciding between calls and folds.
- Overrating the attacker’s continuity as a signal to raise.
- Calling the layer call-heavy because of sporadic blocker calls.
- Misreading partial caps as stable rather than brittle.
