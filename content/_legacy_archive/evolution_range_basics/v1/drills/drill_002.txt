# What to do
Evaluate the provided open raise profile and classify it as linear, polar, or mixed based on continuity and distribution cues.

# Input
Open raise range: AKo, AQo, AQo, KQo, KJo, QJo, AJs, KTs, QTs, JTs, T9s, 98s, 87s, 76s, 65s, 54s, 44, 33, 22 (weighted toward top pairs and suited connectors but no dedicated bluffs).

# Expected reasoning
Linear ranges concentrate on top pairs and connected broadways that can be played for value; look for continuity without wide low equity combos. Polar distributions split between strong value and far bluff combos, often with blockers at the bottom. Mixed or merged ranges blend mid-strength connectors with some bluff coverage but keep a stable value core. Evaluate the lack of explicit bluff-heavy holdings, tight stack of aces, and presence of mid-pocket pairs; this points to a structure without low-equity deep bluffs.

# Correct answer
The profile is linear: top-end pairs plus connected broadways and suited connectors dominate, with no separated bluff layer, so treat it as a coverage-heavy, continuity-focused range.

# Common mistakes
- Calling it polar because the range includes suited connectors.
- Mistaking the mid-pairs for a bluff segment.
- Ignoring the absence of low-equity hands and labeling it merged.
- Confusing two-pair density with broadway weight and calling it linear.
