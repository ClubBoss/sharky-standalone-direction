# Cheatsheet

## Key Rules
- Tight shapes from UTG/MP, widen from CO/BTN, and let SB operate raise-or-fold while BB mixes wider calls.
- Initiative holders defend with small c-bets on dry textures and reload pressure when the board keeps their range strong.
- Without initiative, defend selectively, wait for texture shifts, and only barrel when the board favors your range.

## Fast Heuristics
- Dry flop + initiative → small 25-33% bets; wet flop with initiative → re-upsize or check-only if draws edge toward opponents.
- Losing initiative means checking more, protecting check range, and not barging into two opponents without equity.

## Quick Numbers
- UTG opens ~2.2bb, typical online; BTN/CO can widen beyond that as players fold behind.
- Bet size families: small c-bets ≈25-33%, half-pot when reloading on dynamic turns, bigger after equities shift.

## Examples (1–2 lines)
- UTG bets small on K72r with initiative, then controls the turn once the board stays static.
