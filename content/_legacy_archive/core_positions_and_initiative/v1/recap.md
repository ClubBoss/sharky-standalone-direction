# Recap

## Key Ideas
- Initiative determines whether you pressure with c-bets or defend; seats matter because UTG/MP stay tight while CO/BTN widen.
- Dry boards let initiative holders lean small while opponents without initiative wait for texture shifts before acting.

## Common Mistakes
- Treating every street the same regardless of who fired last and losing fold equity when initiative flips.
- Over-pressuring from early seats or over-defending without initiative, which leaves you bloated against compact ranges.

## Quick Checks
- Why do leaders pick compact bet families on dry boards when they keep initiative?
- How should defense change when you are not the last aggressor?

## Mini-Exercise (1–2 lines)
- Scenario: CO facing BTN and SB flats with no initiative on a K72r flop; note how you defend and when you wait for texture changes.
