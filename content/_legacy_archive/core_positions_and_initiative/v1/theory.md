# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- In 6-max Hold’em, seat dictates [[term:RANGE_ADVANTAGE]] width and discretion; initiative (last pre-street aggressor) lets you push for value with c-bets, while losing it means you defend prudently until texture shifts.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens 2.2bb, CO and BTN call, flop K72rainbow checks to UTG; he bets small to use initiative and fold capped draws, then navigates turn with pot control when the board stays dry.
- Initiative gave UTG credence to pressure; CO/BTN had to defend with caution because they faced multiple streets of aggression without the last bet.

## Actionable Rules
- Tight shapes from UTG/MP, widen on CO and BTN; SB should raise-or-fold and BB mixes wider calls based on opponent tendencies.
- When you have initiative, favor compact c-bets on dry boards and reload pressure on turns that keep your range strong.
- Without initiative, defend selectively, bet only when texture flips in your favor, and avoid barreling into balanced ranges.
- Remember showdown order: after a called river bet the bettor shows first; if it checks through the first active left of the button shows.
