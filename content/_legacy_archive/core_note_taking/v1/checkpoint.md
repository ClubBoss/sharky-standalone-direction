# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Capture quick, structured notes to preserve action, texture, size, and result without pausing play.
- Tag opponents with stable traits, record position/size, and route each hand to fix_now, study_spot, or player_read.
- Analyze hands later to convert insights into fixes without sacrificing session focus.

## Self-Assessment
- I can describe how I tag hands and send them to the right study bucket.
- I keep note capture lean so I do not miss new action.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- You face a K72r flop, call small, fold the turn; note the tag you add and the bucket you route it to.

## Reflection
- Notes should help you act, not slow you down; keep them atomic, searchable, and routed so review time stays efficient.
