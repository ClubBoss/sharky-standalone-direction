# Recap

## Key Ideas
- The turn is the costly inflection point where equity shifts, blockers, and pot geometry dictate whether you barrel again or slow down.
- Match sizing to purpose: big bets deny equity on dynamic cards, small bets hold value when your range is still ahead, and plan the river SPR before the turn ends.

## Common Mistakes
- Auto-barreling every turn after a flop c-bet, which wastes chips on cards that favor the caller.
- Using a single turn size; the wrong size flips equity and leaves bluffs without fold equity.

## Quick Checks
- When should you fire a second barrel instead of checking the turn?
- How does pot geometry guide your turn sizing to set up the river?

## Mini-Exercise (1–2 lines)
- Identify a turn card that hurts your opponent and describe whether you bet big or delay.
