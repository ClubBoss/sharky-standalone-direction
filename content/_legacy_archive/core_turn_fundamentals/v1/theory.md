# Theory Micro-Loop


## Key Idea
- The turn is the inflection point where equity shifts, sizes grow, and pot geometry must plan the river; align each barrel with the range shifts, [[term:BLOCKERS]], and river setup you need.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens, BTN calls, flop K72r. UTG c-bets small, BTN calls. Turn A two-tone boosts UTG range and adds Ax [[term:BLOCKERS]], so UTG fires bet_big_turn to pressure Kx/draws and frame the river.

## Actionable Rules
- Barrel when the turn helps your range or hurts theirs; back off when the card ties the caller into better combos.
- Size by purpose: use bet_big_turn on dynamic cards that change best-hand ranks, and bet_small_turn to deny [[term:EQUITY_DENIAL]] while keeping [[term:THIN_VALUE]].
- Bluff more with [[term:BLOCKERS]] that prevent top pairs or draws, and bluff less when your hand unblocks their continuing range.
- Check back medium-strength hands in position when reverse implied odds are high, or delay c-bets after a flop check to add equity or preserve fold equity.
- Plan pot geometry: set_up_river_jam with strong lines, and size_down_to_see_river when you just need a clean river decision.
