# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Turns demand purposeful barrels; bet big on cards that shift equities and small when value is thin.
- Blockers signal better bluffs while unblocking makes bluffing dangerous.
- Pot geometry must support your river plan—keep SPR in range for your intended finish.

## Self-Assessment
- I can cite when to fire a second barrel versus slowing down.
- I choose turn size based on texture, opponent range, and river SPR.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- After a small flop c-bet on K72r, the turn brings an Ace; explain your sizing and follow-up.

## Reflection
- Respect the turn as a fork in the road: choose barrels that keep opponents guessing and never barrel without a plan.
