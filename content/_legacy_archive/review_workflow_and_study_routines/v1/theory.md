# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- A tight study loop captures hands, triages them, runs a compact solver baseline, locks one knob, creates drills, and schedules spaced review so practice stays focused.

## Mini-Example
- Hand 1 (BTN SRP K72r) tags size_down_dry family and becomes a static sizing drill; Hand 2 (CO missed probe) adds a probe_turns reminder; Hand 3 (BB 3-bet pot JT9ss) locks the size_up_wet turn plan and notes blocker-driven river discipline.

## Actionable Rules
- Capture HU SRP, 3-bet pots, dynamic turns, polar rivers, and price errors with filters that keep hits high-value.
- Triage by price, then family (size_down_dry vs size_up_wet), probe chances, and blocker gates.
- Follow tree discipline (9/12/21/24 and 33/50/75 ladders), read EV/frequencies first, and accept tiny EV mixes earlier.
- Sweep one knob when EV gains persist, keep the lock simple, tag overfold_exploit when the read holds, and re-anchor when the table shifts.
- Annotate one line per street with texture, family, position, initiative, blockers, sequence, pressure gate, and SPR band, ending each line with exactly one token.
- Review on a spaced cadence (fresh → 1d → 3d → 7d) and replace card text with texture/blocker tags.
- Track KPIs (missed probe_turns, wrong family, undisciplined big bets, skipped protect checks) and turn every hand into one demo and one drill with a single token answer.
- Prep sessions with a warmup checklist and top three priorities, then close with a cooldown snapshot and next-session plan.
