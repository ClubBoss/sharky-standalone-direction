# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Dry textures let the initiative holder use compact bets while still extracting value.
- Wet, paired, and monotone boards need larger bets or selective checks, defending only with real equity or blockers.
- Dynamic turn/river cards trigger a shift to bigger bets to protect the hand and deny draws.

## Self-Assessment
- I can match bet sizes to texture and keep initiative advantages on dry boards.
- I adjust my aggression when the board turns dynamic or when the opponent starts showing stronger ranges.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- You lead on T98tt with a small percentage of the range; explain your size choice and the follow-up if the turn adds a flush draw.

## Reflection
- Keep bet sizes tied to real equity and range leverage instead of defaulting to one standard across every board.
