# Recap

## Key Ideas
- Dry, wet, paired, and monotone textures shift which range has leverage, so sizing and plans must align with the board.
- Small bets on dry flops preserve equity while wet or dynamic boards demand larger pressure or selective checking to stop draws.

## Common Mistakes
- Treating K72r and T98tt the same and letting draws realize equity on the wet board.
- Over-committing on paired/monotone textures, letting stronger combos call heavy sizes.

## Quick Checks
- What sizing family fits a dry flop with initiative, and how do you follow up if the turn stays quiet?
- How do you adjust when a dynamic turn forces you to protect against draws?

## Mini-Exercise (1–2 lines)
- Pick a size for a K72r flop with range advantage and explain your sizing plan if a turn adds a second high card.
