# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- Board texture (dry vs wet, paired/monotone) decides who keeps [[term:RANGE_ADVANTAGE]] leverage, so sizing and plans must match.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens, BTN calls, flop is K72r. UTG keeps the lead with tight range and small c-bets, using the static board to finish with another [[term:THIN_VALUE]] bet.
- Same players, flop T98 two-tone. The wet board lets BTN call or raise; UTG upsizes to deny [[term:EQUITY]] and force marginal folds.

## Actionable Rules
- Dry flop → bet smaller/frequently, trust [[term:RANGE_ADVANTAGE]], and fold out overcards without bloating the pot.
- Wet flop → lean on larger bets or selective checking, defending only with real [[term:EQUITY]] or nut [[term:BLOCKERS]].
- Paired/monotone → focus on single pair or high overpairs, avoid huge sizes that invite stronger combos to stack.
- Dynamic turn/river → shift to bigger bets with a plan; static boards keep bets smaller since equity war is slower.
