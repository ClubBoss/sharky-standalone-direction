# Cheatsheet

## Key Rules
- Dry flops invite frequent small bets; trust the static board to realize equity.
- Wet or paired textures demand larger bets or selective checks, defending only with real equity or nut blockers.
- When turns or rivers move adversarially, increase bet sizes to deny draws and protect thin value.

## Fast Heuristics
- K72r: small 25-33% bets and slow-case turns maintain leverage.
- T98tt: lean toward 60-100% bets or checks that force opponents to fold draws.
- Paired/monotone: avoid bloated sizings that invite better hands to stack.

## Quick Numbers
- Small c-bets ≈ 25-33% pot on dry boards.
- Wet/dynamic boards often require 60-100% sizing or selective tree blocks.

## Examples (1–2 lines)
- On K72r, keep small, rhythmic bets and only go bigger if a turn adds new action.
