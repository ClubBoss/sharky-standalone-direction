What it is
Live speech and timing cues converted into consistent frequency shifts using a fixed action set. We never change trees or sizes. Preflop ladders stay 3bet_ip_9bb / 3bet_oop_12bb / 4bet_ip_21bb / 4bet_oop_24bb; postflop families are small_cbet_33, half_pot_50, big_bet_75. Physics first: size_down_dry on static boards; size_up_wet on dynamic boards. Cues only shift frequencies.

Why it matters
Speech and timing compress weak information into actionable tendencies. Used correctly, they nudge how often you take a tokenized line (not which sizes exist). This protects execution under pressure and aligns with population priors (e.g., under-bluffed polar rivers).

Rules of thumb
- Snap small on static flops (range-bet vibe). Treat as merged. Defend now and plan probe_turns after chk-chk.
- Tank->small. Often merged. Call more, raise bluffs less; stick to size_down_dry on static when you bet (small_cbet_33).
- Tank->big. Usually polar and under-bluffed live. Without strong blockers, fold vs big_bet_75.
- Shaky verbal ("just call"). Weak/merged. Value-target with half_pot_50; avoid thin raises.
- Confident forward motion on a dynamic turn + your blockers: double_barrel_good; upgrade to big_bet_75 only with persistent Fv75; otherwise half_pot_50.
- Silence/hollywooding then big: weight to value; fold more without blockers.
- Reverse-tell chatter: low reliability. Require repetition before shifts; overfold_exploit only after persistence.
- Raise-prone turns at mid SPR: prefer delay_turn; earlier add protect_check_range.
- After snap 33 on static then chk-chk: the turn stab is probe_turns.
- Repeated fast folds to your 12bb 3-bets: with blockers, widen 3bet_oop_12bb (or 3bet_ip_9bb) only after multiple occurrences.
- If cues conflict, slow down. Keep family by texture; when uncertain, default to half_pot_50 merged value or small_cbet_33.



Mini example
1) BTN vs BB, SRP K72r (static). IP snaps 33%. You defend. Next orbit villain checks back flop; tag sequence and fire probe_turns on a good turn. 
2) CO vs BTN 3-bet pot on JT9ss (dynamic), turn Qx. Villain speaks confidently and you hold key blockers. Choose double_barrel_good with half_pot_50; upgrade to big_bet_75 only if prior data shows higher Fv75. 
3) River A94r-6s-Kd, villain tanks then bets big_bet_75. Without blockers you fold; with top blockers as PFA, you may consider triple_barrel_scare.

Common mistakes
Over-trusting single hands; mislabeling probe_turns vs probe_turns; inventing off-tree sizes due to adrenaline; calling polar river bets without blockers; ignoring raise-prone opponents on turns; tagging overfold_exploit from one sample.

Mini-glossary
Snap: near-instant action suggesting autopilot or preplanned range. 
Tank->small: long pause then small bet; commonly merged. 
Tank->big: long pause then large bet; commonly polar. 
Blockers: cards that reduce value combos or unblock bluffs. 
Probe_turns: bet turn after flop chk-chk. 
Merged vs polar: medium-strength value vs nutted/air split. 
Repetition tiers: weak(1-2), medium(3-5), strong(6+) events before exploits. 
Under-bluffed river: population tendency to lack enough bluffs on large river bets.

EV: Expected Value - the average amount you'd win or lose if you made the same play many times
