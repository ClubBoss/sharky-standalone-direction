# Theory Micro-Loop

## Key Idea
- Position dictates opening ranges, baits, and blocker deals; tight early, wider late, mix value 3-bets with blocker scrapes, and let stack depth influence marginal hands.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens 2.2 bb, CO 3-bets A5s to 7 bb as a blocker bluff, and UTG responds with KK 4-bet. CO folds because the early range stays strong and the blocker plan is ready.

## Actionable Rules
- Open tight from UTG/MP, widen on CO and BTN because fewer players remain and your steal equity rises.
- Stick to 2.0-2.5 bb opens to keep steal ranges wide while still denying profitable 3-bets.
- Value 3-bet KK+/AK; mix few blocker 3-bets or 4-bets (A5s, A4s) mainly in late-position battles.
- Use stack depth: open small pairs/SCs from CO/BTN with deep stacks, fold them OOP versus big 3-bets.
- SB should race or fold; completing gives BB cheap equity so polarize 3-bets.
- BB defends wider against small raises (2-2.5 bb) and tightens when facing 3-4 bb opens.

## Quick Check
- Why is UTG range tighter than BTN, and how does that affect your 3-bet plan?
- When do blocker bluffs (A5s type) earn a seat in your 3-bet or 4-bet mix?
