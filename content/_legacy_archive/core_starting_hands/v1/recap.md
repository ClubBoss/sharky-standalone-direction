# Recap

## Key Ideas
- Position controls opening shapes: UTG/MP stay tight while CO/BTN widen because fewer players remain and steal equity grows.
- Maintain standard opens (2.0-2.5 bb), mix value 3-bets (KK+/AK) with occasional blocker bluffs (A5s, A4s), and let stack depth guide connectors.

## Common Mistakes
- Opening BTN ranges from UTG, which leaves you OOP against multiple players with dominated hands.
- Calling 3-bets OOP with marginal offsuit broadways instead of folding or using blocker 4-bets.

## Quick Checks
- Why keep UTG tighter than BTN, and how does it affect your 3-bet mix?
- When do blocker bluffs like A5s deserve a 3-bet or 4-bet action?

## Mini-Exercise (1–2 lines)
- You are CO facing UTG open; note how you size a 3-bet with blockers versus value.
