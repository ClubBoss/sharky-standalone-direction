What it is
Live chip handling and declarations translated into reliable execution. Procedures keep us inside fixed ladders and size families (33/50/75 postflop; 9/12/21/24 preflop). Physics first: size_down_dry on static boards; size_up_wet on dynamic boards. Chip-handling cues only shift [[term:FREQUENCY]].

Why it matters
Clean declarations prevent illegal or mis-sized bets that create off-tree chaos and [[term:TILT]]. Staying within 33/50/75 keeps cognition free for texture-driven plans and river discipline, lowering [[term:VARIANCE]] and error rate while keeping the same trees.

Rules of thumb
* Oversized chip rule: one big chip without "raise" is a call; say "raise" first. Why: avoid unintended sizes.
* Cut stacks, count, then bet. Pre-choose small_cbet_33 / half_pot_50 / big_bet_75; never splash the pot. Why: family adherence.
* Line rule / one motion: know if chips crossing the line are binding. In strict single_motion_raise_legal rooms, add protect_check_range / delay_turn. Why: avoid traps.
* Incomplete/min raise: ensure legal thresholds; else default to closest family size. Why: no invented sizes.
* Out_of_turn: freeze, let action back up, re-evaluate texture; keep the family. Why: preserve plan.
* Side pots: when all-ins create side pots, confirm amounts verbally; stick to family. Why: clarity.
* Show-order: use bettor_shows_first / first_active_left_of_btn_shows to bank info; later probe_turns after chk-chk. Why: convert procedure into edges.
* Rivers: tank->big is under-bluffed live; fold without [[term:BLOCKERS]]. With [[term:BLOCKERS]] as PFA on scares, triple_barrel_scare. Why: population.

Live overlay integration
* Strict enforcement: increase protect_check_range and delay_turn in raise-prone nodes; families unchanged.
* Loose enforcement: expect more stabs; protect_check_range more; default half_pot_50 over thin polar lines.
* table_speed low or fatigue high: simplify to small_cbet_33 / half_pot_50; reduce triple_barrel_scare.
* avg_stack_bb low: lower SPR favors half_pot_50; upgrade to big_bet_75 only with blockers + fold trend.

Mini example
BTN vs BB, single_motion_raise_legal strict. Flop K72r (static): you verbalize "thirty-three" and place chips cleanly -> small_cbet_33 (size_down_dry). Turn 5x; with raise-prone IP, choose delay_turn.
Another hand: CO vs BTN. Villain tosses one oversized chip without saying "raise"; you confirm it is a call, keep plan on dynamic T98ss, then with blockers on 7x choose double_barrel_good using half_pot_50; river tank->big from tight reg without blockers -> fold.

Common mistakes
Splashing chips into unintended sizes; forgetting oversized-chip = call; mislabeling probe vs probe_turns; arguing line_rule instead of asking the floor; hero-calling polar rivers; skipping protect_check_range in loose rooms; changing sizes mid-hand after an out_of_turn reset.

Mini-glossary
String bet: multiple chip pushes without clear declaration.
Single-motion raise: one continuous forward motion; required in many rooms.
Line rule: chips crossing the line may be binding.
Verbal binding: words define the bet if clear.
Oversized chip: one big chip counts as call unless "raise" is declared.
Incomplete raise: insufficient increase; may be forced to minimum raise or ruled a call.
Side pot: pot created when one or more players are all-in.
Splash the pot: messy chip throw; discouraged/illegal; avoid.

[[term:EV]]: Expected Value - the average amount you'd win or lose if you made the same play many times
