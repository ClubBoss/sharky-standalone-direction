# Scenario
IP fires a mid-size turn stab after a flop adoption, leaving OOP to determine if the widening density gap and creeping pressure demand a fold-window continuation.

# Snapshot
Attacker retains light advantage, density gap opens around the defender’s mids, pressure tier climbs to mid/high, fragility surfaces, and defender reactions drift from calls toward fold-heavy responses.

# Player task
Identify the correct turn heuristic chain and explain how those cues guide the next move.

# Expected reasoning
Structure keeps the advantage while the density gap shows the defender leaking combos. Pressure escalates into mid/high tiers, and fragility warns the defender is losing coverage. Reaction layers slide toward fold-heavy behavior, so folding replaces continued calls. The chain therefore points to a fold window, not a timing or thin-value read, because the node now supports decisive pressure. Aligning structure, density, pressure, reaction, and window verifies the fold-window chain before betting.

# Correct line
Advantage → density gap → high pressure → fold-heavy reaction → fold window.

# Why not the alternatives
- Confusing timing with a fold window. 
- Overvaluing blockers while fragility grows. 
- Ignoring the density shift and staying passive. 
- Forcing thin value when pressure already peaks. 
- Treating a mid-pressure tier as low. 
- Projecting flop assumptions onto this turn.
