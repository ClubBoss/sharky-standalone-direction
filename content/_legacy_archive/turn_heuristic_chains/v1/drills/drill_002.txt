# What to do
Choose whether the cues describe a timing-window chain or a thin-value-pressure chain.

# Input
Advantage stays mild, the density edge is narrow, the pressure tier sits mid, reactions drift call-heavy, and fragility exposure remains low.

# Expected reasoning
Structure keeps slight leverage while the narrow gap is the only softness.
Mid-tier pressure allows probing but not blasting; the defender keeps calling steadily.
Low fragility resists collapse, so thin-value pressure lacks support until fragility grows.
The chain therefore favors a timing window until reactions soften further and the node relaxes.

# Correct answer
Timing-window chain: the defender still calls and fragility is minimal.

# Common mistakes
- Misreading call-heavy cues as fold-heavy.
- Overestimating fragility to justify pressure.
- Confusing timing windows with stab windows.
- Flattening the narrow density edge.
- Forcing high-pressure reads without confirmation.
- Projecting flop assumptions onto the turn.
