# What it is
Turn heuristic chains string together structure, density, pressure, reactions, fragility, and windows so every turn cue feeds the next move.

# Why it matters
The turn is EV-critical because ranges tighten and pressure spikes. The chain keeps you from overshooting fragile zones, lets thin-value or timing windows open, and aligns aggression with the opponent’s evolving coverage.

# Rules of thumb
- Identify the advantage shift after the flop.
- Reassess density gaps to see whether weakness or resilience appears.
- Update the pressure tier before choosing action.
- Track fragility transitions to guard sensitive combos.
- Read reaction-layer changes to spot whether calls, folds, or raises dominate.
- Ensure window alignment before you bet.
- Keep tempo steady so sudden rushes do not break the chain.
- Skip thin pressure without a clear window.

# Mini example
Attacker holds heavier top-end density and layered mids while the defender is semi-capped with a fragile segment. The chain sees advantage, a mid pressure tier, call-heavy reactions, and a thin-value window forming, so it waits before pressuring.

# Common mistakes
- Chasing aggression without confirming the pressure tier.
- Treating every density gap as exploitable.
- Ignoring reaction shifts and expecting folds.
- Overcommitting thin pressure without window alignment.
- Breaking tempo before the chain confirms the path.
- Forcing pressure on defenders that keep the protection band tight.
- Confusing fleeting dominance with lasting advantage.
- Forgetting to restart the chain after new action.

-# Workflow
- Note the turn advantage relative to the flop.
- Remap density to spot tightened or leaking areas.
- Recompute the pressure tier after the latest action.
- Read reaction layers for the defender’s dominant mix.
- Track fragility so weak combos stay guarded.
- Sync windows with these signals before betting.
- Hold tempo until the chain confirms the step.

# Mini-glossary
- heuristic chain : linked cues guiding turn play.
- density shape : how concentrated remaining combos are.
- pressure tier : the aggression level the node can sustain.
- reaction layer : the defender’s mix of calls, folds, raises.
- fragility zone : sensitive segments that collapse under pressure.
- window alignment : matching aggression to fold equity.
- tempo consistency : a steady rhythm that respects the chain.
- timing window : a short-lived pocket for conditional aggression.
- thin-value lane : a low-pressure path to extract value.
- exploit seed : the early sign of a leak.

# EV
Turn heuristic chains stabilize EV by forcing each adjustment to honor layered signals. They keep thin-value and timing windows credible, prevent pressure from collapsing fragile zones, and make the turn predictable instead of a high-variance minefield.
