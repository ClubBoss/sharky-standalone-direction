# Scenario
IP opened with a small flop advantage, and the turn card nudged the top-end density upward without blowing the range. Pressure climbs from low to mid/high as you choose between a small follow-up or a larger bet that forces folds. Defender loses some strong combos, mid-band softens, and reactions bifurcate: one sizing branch drift fold-heavy while the other keeps calling.

# Snapshot
Flop left you slightly ahead with layered tops. Turn adds more toppers and tightens mids. Small bets hold moderate pressure; larger bets elevate the tier. Defender fragility rises, yet part of the range tolerates pressure, keeping call-heavy lines alive while another portion dims toward folding.

# Player task
Name the main turn heuristic chain and choose whether timing-window, fold-window, or thin-value-pressure is the primary plan.

# Expected reasoning
Structure keeps advantage while density grows modestly. Mid/high pressure tier means aggressive sizing must respect fragility. Reaction layers split: bigger bets push defenders toward folds, small bets leave them calling. That duality matches a fold-window chain triggered by the larger sizing once fragility opens.

# Correct line
Fold-window chain tied to the larger sizing; smaller bets keep the timing window warm until the defender leans toward folding.

# Common misreads
- Treating all reactions as call-heavy and missing the fold-heavy branch.
- Confusing flop pressure with the new turn tier.
- Overvaluing blockers instead of watching fragility.
- Ignoring fragility and betting too meekly.
- Forcing thin-value pressure when the fold window needs the larger sizing.
- Calling it full advantage instead of a controlled density edge.
