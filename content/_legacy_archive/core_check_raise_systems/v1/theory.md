# Theory Micro-Loop


## Key Idea
- [[term:CHECK_RAISE]] systems blend value and bluffs based on texture, sizing, and future turn plans so you protect your checking range and punish aggression.

## Mini-Example
- SB checks K72r after CO bets 33%. SB small check-raises with K7s for value, keeps weaker Ks in, and then upsizes on the 5 two-tone turn to deny draws while shaping the river.

## Actionable Rules
- Value [[term:CHECK_RAISE]] on dynamic boards with two pair+, sets, or strong draws that can barrel profitably.
- Bluff with [[term:BLOCKERS]]/backdoor draws that improve on many turns and avoid unblocking your opponent's best combos.
- Size small on dry boards to fold out air and keep worse pairs in; size big on wet boards to charge [[term:EQUITY]] and set up the river.
- Lean on position/range: fewer bluffs vs early-position c-bets, more vs late-position steals.
- Plan the turn before you [[term:CHECK_RAISE]]: barrel when cards improve you or punish them, slow down on bricks that favor the bettor.

## Quick Check
- When do you prefer a small vs big [[term:CHECK_RAISE]], and what turn cards keep the plan alive?
- Why is it critical to guard your checking range with the right combination of value and bluff combos?
