### C18 Recaps: Cross-Spot Integration

The C18 recap consolidates the C13- C17 playbooks into a single, cross-spot routine. You combine exploit plan building, mixed checkpoints, and stage-aware adjustments to keep decisions consistent across SRP and 3BP, in or out of position (UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB). The goal is to spot leaks, pick the right conversion, and carry the plan through turn and river without drift.

Start with positioning and stack context. In SRP IP on dry high boards, cbet_small remains the baseline from earlier modules, exploiting range edge and overfolding blinds. In 3BP OOP on dynamic boards, you borrow the mixed checkpoints: check more, deploy cbet_big only with strong equity or clean blockers. Stack depth dictates leverage: deeper stacks support multi-barrel pressure; shallow stacks favor simplified lines and more fold-or-raise decisions.

Population leaks repeat. Overfolding fields at ICM payjumps invite wide opens and second barrels. Overcalling blinds require thinner value with raise or cbet_big on wet textures. Overbluffing regulars in delayed pots push you toward check-call lines with bluff-catchers. Undersized cbets are still fear signals; raise or float then pressure favorable turns. Every exploit still depends on a trigger; log the cue and pick an action conversion (check_back to probe_bet, call to raise, fold to call when ranges overfold).

Board and street integration carry over from mixed checkpoints. Flop: define board class and choose size. Turn: ask whether the card helps you or villain and whether stack-to-pot allows a credible barrel. River: decide between thin value and shutdown by counting worse hands that call and bluffs you beat. Keep the same questions for SRP and 3BP so the flow stays automatic under time pressure.

Position shifts need explicit notes. IP with nut advantage: lean on small sizing to print folds and keep range wide. OOP without nut edge: control pot, defend with bluff-catchers that block value, and use probe_bet when villain skips cbets. BTN and SB steal more when blinds overfold; UTG and MP tighten under ICM. BB defense widens versus small sizes but respects large barrels on scary turns.

Cross-spot heuristics tie everything together. If the board pairs and population overcalls, trim bluffs and value bet thinner. If the turn bricks after a small flop bet, a repeat small size works both IP and OOP when ranges stayed wide. If a scare card favors you, size up; if it favors villain, slow down or check. When aggression appears out of character (sudden large river bet from passive player), weight folding unless you hold strong blockers.

Contrast with GTO: the balanced path would mix lines evenly; the recap biases toward identified leaks and recent evidence while keeping enough protection to avoid being exploited back.

To execute: set the preflop context, tag the board, choose the flop size, define turn branches for bricks versus scares, and set river thresholds for value versus shutdown. Use the same loop for SRP and 3BP, IP and OOP. By reusing the earlier modules' triggers and conversions, you get a stable, deterministic routine that turns observed leaks into consistent profit without losing control of pot size or bluff frequency. The recap is a checklist you can replay every orbit, keeping your decisions tight, fast, and grounded in the combined C13- C17 logic.
