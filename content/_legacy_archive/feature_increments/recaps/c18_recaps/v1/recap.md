### C18 Recaps Summary

The C18 recap stitches together exploit plans, checkpoints, and stage-aware adjustments from C13 through C17. Start every hand by tagging pot type (SRP or 3BP), position (UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB), and stack depth. In SRP IP on dry boards, keep cbet_small as the baseline to leverage range edge and overfolding tendencies. In 3BP OOP on dynamic boards, check more and reserve cbet_big for strong equity or clean blockers. Deep stacks allow multi-barrel pressure; shallow stacks call for simplified, low-risk conversions.

Board texture anchors your plan. Dry, high boards favor small, frequent cbets. Wet textures push you to bigger bets or selective checks. Paired boards reduce bluff success, so delay aggression or check_back with medium strength. When turns brick after small flop bets, repeat small to fold ace-highs. When scare cards favor you, size up or raise small leads; when they favor villain, slow down. Rivers demand discipline: thin value into overcalling pools; otherwise, check when ranges are bluff-catch heavy and villains under-bluff.

Population leaks stay central. Overfolding blinds or ICM fear invite wider opens and second barrels. Overcalling players reward thinner value and larger sizing on wet textures. Overbluffing regs after checks push you to check-call with bluff-catchers that block value. Undersized cbets in pressure spots cap ranges; raise or float then pressure on bricks. Pair each exploit trigger with a clear conversion.

Cross-spot heuristics keep the routine portable. IP with nut edge: small sizes and steady barrels. OOP without nut edge: pot control and targeted raises against weak sizing. Turn checkpoints ask whether the card helps you or them and whether stack-to-pot supports a credible bluff. River checkpoints ask how many worse hands call and how many bluffs you beat.
