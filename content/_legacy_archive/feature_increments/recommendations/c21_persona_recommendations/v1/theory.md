### C21 Persona-Aware Recommendations

Persona-aware planning adapts study flow to the learner's traits and current emotion state. Some players crave rapid drills, others need structured checklists. Tier-A emotion signals like calm, focus, and tension help weight what to serve next: calm favors deeper theory or harder drills, focus aligns with timed micro-quizzes, and tension suggests shorter, confidence-building reps. By linking persona traits with recent accuracy trends, the recommendation engine keeps practice productive without overload.

[[IMAGE: c21_persona_flow | persona recommendation flow]]

Start with a persona profile: preferred session length, tolerance for uncertainty, and appetite for aggression. A cautious persona gets more SRP dry-board reps with cbet_small focus and fewer complex 3BP OOP spots early. An assertive persona receives more probe_bet and raise practice, especially against undersized cbets. Session-length heuristics keep engagement stable: 10-15 minute blocks for tension-prone personas, 20-30 minute blocks for calm grinders, and mixed short sprints for focus-heavy but variance-sensitive players.

Emotion states adjust weights dynamically. Calm expands difficulty: mix advanced multi-street drills, add thin value scenarios, and stretch to longer spacing windows (7-14 days). Focus favors timed micro-quizzes with immediate feedback, reinforcing rapid recognition of range edge, nut edge, and blocker support. Tension scales back: serve review of recent misses, overfold/overcall tags, and simple SRP IP lines that restore confidence. If accuracy dips under a threshold, reduce complexity until streaks recover.

Matching difficulty to accuracy trends keeps momentum. Accuracy above 85% unlocks harder drills: 3BP OOP dynamic boards, river bluff-catch decisions versus overbluff tags, or thin value versus overcallers. At 70-85%, stay in mixed difficulty with clear hints and smaller pot scenarios. Below 70%, switch to foundational micro-quizzes and single-street drills with cbet_small, check_back, and basic raise versus undersized cbet. Every recommendation logs outcome and updates the next review window, following the spacing ladder (1h, 1d, 3d, 7d, 14d).

The engine ingests C13 through C20 results. From C13 (exploit planning), it learns which triggers you spot and which conversions you miss. From C14-C16 mixed checkpoints, it notes whether you manage multi-street shifts. From C17 recaps, it measures cross-spot consistency. From C18 micro-quizzes and C19 density drills, it tracks speed and recall under short prompts. From C20 spaced repetition, it reads which intervals work for you and which tags keep resurfacing. The combination yields a persona-tuned queue.

Recommendations also respect position and pot type. If a persona struggles OOP in 3BP, the system schedules more check-call and probe_bet reps with blockers. If IP SRP accuracy is high, it stretches to tougher river nodes and thin value bets. Leak tags guide content: overfold issues get more bluff-catch drills; overcall issues get value/thin value calibration; overbluff issues get fold discipline; undersized cbet issues get raise/float practice with clear blockers.

Live versus online adjustments matter. Live players have lower volume and higher emotional swings; recommendations bias toward shorter blocks and immediate post-session review. Online grinders can handle longer sets with more timed micro-quizzes and spaced intervals during breaks. Both paths keep the same spacing ladder but tweak block length and content weight.

Positions stay explicit: UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB remain anchors for every plan. Contrast with GTO: a balanced baseline spreads actions evenly, while persona-aware recommendations bias toward current leaks and emotion state. Contrast with a static study plan: static sequences ignore persona and mood, leading to boredom or overwhelm. Persona-aware recommendations stay deterministic but adaptive: they pick the next item based on persona traits, Tier-A emotion state, accuracy trend, leak tags, and spacing window. The result is a stable loop that keeps study efficient, confidence steady, and poker heuristics ready under pressure.
