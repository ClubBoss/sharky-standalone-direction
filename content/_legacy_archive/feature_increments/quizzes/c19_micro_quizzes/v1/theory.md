### C19 Micro-Quizzes: High-Density Reinforcement

Micro-quizzes sharpen recall by compressing key heuristics into tiny checkpoints. You train to spot position (UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB), pot type (SRP or 3BP), and board texture in one glance, then choose the simplest correct action. The format is dense on purpose: one or two lines per idea so you can review fast and expose misconceptions. This module reuses the exploit, checkpoint, and recap logic from prior sets but shrinks them into rapid prompts.

Each micro prompt should answer three things: who has range edge, who has nut edge, and what the default size or action is. Dry high boards in SRP IP still favor cbet_small. Dynamic boards in 3BP OOP still reward checking more and reserving cbet_big for strong equity or clean blockers. Paired boards reduce bluff success, so check_back medium strength unless population overfolds. When a scare card helps you, size up; when it helps villain, slow down. Stack depth still matters: shallow stacks bias toward fold or jam decisions, while deeper stacks allow layered barrels and thin value.

Misconception correction is the main goal. Overfolding to small cbets on dry boards loses EV; the right answer is often to continue with backdoors in BB. Overcalling paired boards without blockers burns chips; the fix is to fold more or choose hands that block trips or top pairs. Raising undersized cbets in fear-heavy spots works when ranges are capped, but spewing versus strong sizing is a leak. Micro prompts force you to tag these patterns quickly: overfold vs overcall vs overbluff vs undersized cbet.

Tiny multi-street heuristics stay consistent. If flop was cbet_small and turn bricks, repeating small often works IP when ranges stay wide. If villain skips a cbet and turn bricks, probe_bet takes initiative. Rivers need clarity: thin value versus overcallers, or check when pools under-bluff. Bluff only when you block value and unblock folds; avoid random bluffs on paired boards or scare cards that help villain. Keep questions short: range edge, nut edge, blocker support, fold equity, pot odds.

IP and OOP micro-rules differ but remain simple. IP with advantage: small sizing, steady barrels, and thin value. OOP without advantage: pot control, check-call with bluff-catchers that block value, and selective probe_bet when villain declines to cbet. In 3BP OOP on wet textures, checking is default; in SRP IP on dry textures, betting small is default. Translate every prompt back to these anchors.

Contrast with GTO: balanced play mixes lines for indifference; micro-quizzes bias you toward population tendencies and clarity over perfect balance. The intent is not to memorize trees but to encode fast, repeatable decisions that exploit common leaks.

Execution checklist for each micro prompt: identify position and pot, tag board class, pick default size (cbet_small or cbet_big) or check_back, set turn reaction for bricks versus scares, and choose river value or shutdown. Confirm whether the leak is overfold, overcall, overbluff, or undersized cbet, then pick the appropriate conversion: check_back to probe_bet, call to raise, fold to call when opponents overfold. Keeping prompts this tight makes practice fast, deterministic, and ready for in-game recall.
