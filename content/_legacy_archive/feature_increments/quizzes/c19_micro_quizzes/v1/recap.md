### C19 Micro-Quizzes Recap

The C19 micro set condenses the prior exploit and checkpoint logic into rapid-fire prompts. Every question forces you to tag position (UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB), pot type (SRP or 3BP), and board class, then pick the simplest correct action. Dry, high boards in SRP IP still favor cbet_small for range edge. Wet, dynamic boards in 3BP OOP still push you to check more and reserve cbet_big for strong equity. Paired boards reduce bluff success, so check_back medium strength unless population overfolds.

Misconception correction is core. Folding too often to small cbets wastes equity; continuing with backdoors in BB fixes it. Overcalling paired boards without blockers leaks chips; fold more or pick hands that block trips or top pairs. Raising undersized cbets is good only when ranges are capped and stacks allow fold equity. Treat each prompt as a leak tag: overfold, overcall, overbluff, or undersized cbet, then pair it with a clear conversion like probe_bet or a thin value bet.

Micro rules stay aligned with earlier modules. If flop was cbet_small and turn bricks, repeating small often works IP when ranges stay wide. If villain skips a cbet and turn bricks, probe_bet takes initiative. Rivers demand clarity: thin value versus overcallers; check when pools under-bluff. Bluff only with blocker support and fold equity; avoid random stabs on paired boards or scare cards that help villain. IP with nut edge: small sizes and steady barrels. OOP without nut edge: pot control, check-call with bluff-catchers, and selective probes when villain declines to cbet.

Contrast with GTO: balanced play mixes lines evenly to reach equilibrium; micro-quizzes bias you toward field errors and clarity. The execution loop stays short: set context, map board, choose flop size, define turn reactions for bricks versus scares, and set river thresholds for value or shutdown. By cycling these in a compact format, you build fast recall and reinforce correct defaults across SRP, 3BP, IP, and OOP spots.
