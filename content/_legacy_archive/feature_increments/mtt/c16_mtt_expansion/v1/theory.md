### C16 MTT Expansion: Exploit Plan Builder

The Exploit Plan Builder for multi-table tournaments turns broad tendencies into practical, stage-aware counter-plans. You start by reading stack depth, payout pressure, and population habits, then map each signal to a clear exploit: value heavier when fields overcall, bluff thinner when they overfold, or punish undersized cbets with raises. The focus is rapid, simple conversions rather than balanced-study trees, because tournament edges come from timely shifts, not perfect equilibrium.

[[IMAGE: c16_map | exploit map]]

Tournament stacks move quickly, so every exploit must anchor to depth. At 60bb effective, you can float more and apply turn pressure; at 25bb, you bias toward straightforward, single-street plans; at 15bb and below, folds and jams replace thin calls. The builder keeps positions explicit (UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB) and ties them to depth bands, so your counter-actions scale with risk and leverage.

ICM adds another axis. Near payjumps, population players overfold in early and middle positions, fearing busts. Your exploit is to expand steals from BTN and SB with raise and cbet_small lines, while trimming bluff-catch calls in BB versus strong-looking opens. When short stacks face pressure, many opponents undersize cbets to control pot; you can convert checks into probe_bet attacks or turn calls into raises against caps.

[[IMAGE: c16_trigger | exploit trigger]]

An exploit trigger is the concrete cue that justifies a shift: a repeated undersized cbet, a showdown showing overbluff, or a fold-to-steal spike in the HUD. Once you see the trigger, apply action conversion: check_back becomes probe_bet, call becomes raise, or fold becomes call when fields overfold. Every conversion should respect stack depth and position; for example, BB versus BTN cbet_small at 30bb can float and pressure turns, while the same spot at 12bb leans fold unless board favors your range.

Population leaks remain consistent across many MTT fields. Overfold: older recreationals fold too often under ICM; response is wider steal and double-barrel bluffing with blockers. Overcall: sticky callers in BB and SB defend wide; answer with thinner value raises and cbet_big on wet boards. Overbluff: some regs triple too much on scare cards; counter with bluff-catch calls that fit pot odds, avoiding unnecessary raises. Undersized cbets: common near bubbles; reply with raise bluffs that punish capped ranges, or probe_bet when they check.

Contrast with a GTO baseline: GTO seeks balance, while the Exploit Plan Builder intentionally biases toward the opponent leak even if it leaves you temporarily unbalanced.

Stage-specific planning keeps you proactive. Early stage with deep stacks: prioritize information gathering and exploit notes; use cbet_small to deny equity but avoid bloating pots without clear edge. Mid stage with 30-40bb: pick lines that force errors, especially versus overfolding blinds. Late stage or bubble: ICM-first logic; trim marginal calls, prefer raises that pressure medium stacks, and attack undersized cbets that signal fear. Final table: stack distance to payjumps dictates aggression; use raise to punish scared money.

The builder's workflow: profile opponent tendencies, tag the exploit trigger, select the action conversion, and execute with sizing that matches depth. Re-evaluate after showdowns to confirm if the trigger persists. By keeping everything in checklists, you ensure repeatable decisions: read stack, read position, detect leak, convert action, and commit. The result is a deterministic system that thrives in live-paced tournaments where each orbit shifts risk and reward.
