### C20 Spaced Repetition Recap

Spaced repetition makes poker habits stick by resurfacing spots just before memory fades. Start with a simple ladder: 1 hour, 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days. Misses drop back to 1 day; clean runs at 14 days can stretch to 21 days. Micro-quizzes feed the scheduler with single-rule prompts like cbet_small on dry boards or check_back on paired boards. Drills add multi-street checkpoints so you practice flop plan, turn adjustment, and river decision in order.

Tag every miss so the scheduler knows what to replay. Use labels for position (UTG, MP, CO, BTN, SB, BB), pot type (SRP, 3BP), board class (dry, dynamic, paired), and leak type (overfold, overcall, overbluff, undersized_cbet). Tags let you resurface clusters: if you miss multiple overbluff responses, you will see more bluff-catch and probe_bet prompts soon. Correct answers move forward; wrong answers just get more reps without penalty.

Spacing differs live versus online. Live volume is lower, so keep reviews tight after play: 1 hour, then 1 day, 3 days, 7 days. Online sessions can insert a 90-minute micro block in-session and still follow the main ladder. Before ICM-heavy events, add a small block of bubble and payjump spots the day before to refresh specific pressure rules.

Maintain the core heuristics while you review. Dry high boards in SRP IP favor cbet_small. Dynamic 3BP OOP boards default to checks with cbet_big only when equity is strong. Paired boards reduce bluff success, so check_back more. Undersized cbets in pressure spots are capped; raise or float then pressure turns. Overcalling pools reward thin value; overfolding pools invite second barrels. Each micro or drill should connect to one of these anchors so the scheduler trains the right reflex.

Contrast with cramming: mass study fades fast. Spacing forces active recall, prioritizes weak items, and keeps total time low. Execute the loop: pick a set, quiz, tag misses, schedule the next window, and keep sessions short. The result is a deterministic, ASCII-only routine that keeps SRP and 3BP, IP and OOP heuristics ready under time pressure.
