# Scenario
IP maintains mid pressure; OOP sits behind caps and must synthesize structure, pressure, and reaction cues before answering.

# Snapshot
Attacker: steady top/mid continuity, layered blockers. Defender: caps on mid band, fragility in the middles, pressure cue: climbing but controlled bets, reaction shift toward call-heavy.

# Player task
Deliver the unified read combining structure, pressure tier, reaction layer, and window.

# Expected reasoning
Continuity keeps mid pressure honest while the caps slow down volleys, so the tier stays mid. Defender’s fragile middles lean toward calls rather than raises under pressure. That reaction opens a timing window before the caps harden or the defenders fold outright. Synthesis demands respecting the structure, anticipating the call-heavy reaction, and seizing the timing window that the compressed pressure tier offers.

# Correct line
Mid pressure + call-heavy → timing window: apply controlled probes before caps react.

# Why not the alternatives
- Fold window: defender still calls under pressure, not folds. 
- Stab window: needs cap breaks that haven’t happened. 
- Thin-value: misreads call-heavy reactions as immediate folds. 
- High pressure: overstates the cap resistance. 
- Disconnected cues: any single signal is insufficient without the synthesis chain.
