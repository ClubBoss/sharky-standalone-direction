# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Pre-session routines and stop-loss/time-box guard your decisions from fatigue and chase tilt.
- Fast interventions (breath reset, short walk) clear the mind without derailing play.
- Tag and log hands quickly for later study instead of replaying them during the session.

## Self-Assessment
- I maintain a checklist and know when to end a session per my stop-loss and time box.
- I can deploy a breathe-reset or quick table change the moment tilt creeps in.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- After a bad beat, you feel chest tightness; describe the 10-second breathe-reset and table adjustments you use.

## Reflection
- Routines protect attention; respect them so variance cannot hijack your execution.
