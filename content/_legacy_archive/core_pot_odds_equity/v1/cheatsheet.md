# Cheatsheet

## Key Rules
- Pot odds = call_amount / (pot + call_amount); compare to your equity to know whether a call pays.
- Use rule of 4 on the flop and rule of 2 on the turn to translate clean outs into equity quickly.
- Count only clean outs: drop cards that pair the board, give opponents better hands, or show as blocked.
- Factor implied odds when future winnings justify a call, and respect reverse implied odds when made hands can still lose.

## Fast Heuristics
- Rule of 4 ≈ outs x 4 on flop, rule of 2 ≈ outs x 2 on turn.
- Clean outs ignore cards that pair the board or are tied to opponents' stronger holdings.

## Quick Numbers
- Example: 9 clean outs on the flop → ~36% equity vs pot odds 20%.
- On turn: 9 outs → ~18% equity.

## Examples (1–2 lines)
- BTN 30 into 120, 9 clean outs: rule of 4 gives 36%, so call because pot odds 20% undercut your equity.
