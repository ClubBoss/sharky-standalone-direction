# Checkpoint

## What You Should Know
- Pot odds compare the call price to the pot to find your breakeven percentage.
- Rule of 4 (flop) and rule of 2 (turn) quickly estimate equity from clean outs.
- Clean outs exclude blockers, board pairs, or cards that give opponents better hands, while implied and reverse implied odds adjust for future streets.

## Self-Assessment
- I can calculate pot odds and compare them to rule-of-thumb equity.
- I only track clean outs and know when implied odds justify a marginal call.

## Quick Scenario (1–2 lines)
- BTN bets 30 into a 120 pot with 9 clean outs; explain whether you call and why.

## Reflection
- Let math guide your calls; only deviate when implied odds or reverse implied risks are clear.
