# Recap

## Key Ideas
- Pot odds compare the call price to the pot so you know the breakeven percentage, and equity estimates show how often you win if all cards drop.
- The rule of 4 (flop) and 2 (turn) gives quick equity estimates from clean outs, while implied and reverse implied odds adjust the math when future streets matter.

## Common Mistakes
- Counting non-clean outs that pair the board, improve opponents, or are blocked, which inflates equity.
- Misapplying the rule of 2/4 (flop multiplier on the turn) and distort your thresholds.

## Quick Checks
- When do you trust pot odds instead of implied odds?
- How do you keep clean outs clean of blockers or pairings?
