# Scenario
OOP defends wide preflop, IP taps a small stab on the flop; the player must judge who retains structural advantage via nut edge and mid cohesion.

# Snapshot
IP: clear nut edge, steady high cards, layered mid coverage. OOP: spread mid-band with loose combos, weaker cohesion, modest top end.

# Player task
Decide which side holds advantage and explain how the nut edge, mid-band spread, and cohesion inform the judgment.

# Expected reasoning
IP’s nut edge and layered mid coverage keep aggression credible while OOP’s spread mids lack cohesion, making it hard to apply pressure. The defender’s broad mid band looks playable, but without cohesion it gives up to initiative, so advantage remains with IP. The cohesion contrast shows which side can maintain structural leverage, and IP’s high cards resist flattening. OOP’s mid spread may bluff, but it lacks the continuity to trade effectively, so IP’s edge holds.

# Correct line
IP has the advantage: nut edge plus cohesive mids keep him in control.

# Why not the alternatives
- OOP advantage: overvalues the spread mid band instead of cohesion.
- Even: ignores IP’s nut edge and layering.
- Defender pressure: assumes spread mids offer consistent threat, which cohesion disproves.
- Polar read: misinterprets OOP’s spread as bluff-heavy when it is reactive.
- Top-end tie: ignores how IP’s cohesion sustains action while OOP’s mid split collapses.
