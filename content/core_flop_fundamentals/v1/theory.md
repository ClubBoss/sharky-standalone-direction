# Flop Fundamentals

## Key Idea
- Flop strategy starts with board texture and range interaction.
- Dry boards usually favor smaller bets more often.
- Wet boards usually need larger bets or more selective checking because many draws can continue.

## Mini-Example
- UTG opens, BTN calls, flop is `K72` rainbow. This board is static, so a small continuation bet is often enough.
- Same players see `T98` two-tone. This board is dynamic, so larger sizing can deny equity and charge draws.

## Working Rules
- On dry boards with a clear range edge, prefer smaller continuation bets.
- On wet or connected boards, use larger bets more selectively and be ready to check more hands.
- On paired or monotone boards, avoid automatic large bets; plan the turn first.
- If the preflop raiser checks back, the caller can sometimes lead the turn when the new card shifts advantage.
- Keep sizing families consistent so your decisions are easier to repeat under pressure.
