# Positions & Initiative

## Key Idea
- Position means acting later in the hand.
- Initiative means you were the last player to bet or raise.
- Acting later and keeping initiative usually make decisions easier and more profitable.

## Mini-Example
- CO opens, BTN calls, flop is `K72` rainbow.
- CO makes a small continuation bet and BTN calls.
- On the turn, CO still has initiative and can choose between a second bet or a check with better information than if CO were out of position.

## Working Rules
- In position, you can control pot size more easily with medium-strength hands.
- Out of position, play tighter and avoid marginal calls that create hard turn and river spots.
- Initiative gives you more ways to win: value bets, bluffs, and delayed pressure.
- Losing initiative does not mean auto-fold; it means you should be clearer about your hand class.
- Preflop seat order matters because it shapes who speaks first on later streets.
