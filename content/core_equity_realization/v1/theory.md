# Equity Realization

## Key Idea
- Equity is your share of the pot in theory.
- Equity realization is how much of that share you actually collect by the end of the hand.
- You usually realize more equity when you have position, initiative, and a clear plan for later streets.

## Definitions
- Definition: Position means acting later than your opponent.
- Definition: Initiative means you were the last player to bet or raise.

## Mini-Example
- CO opens and BTN calls. Flop is `K72` rainbow.
- CO uses a small continuation bet. BTN calls.
- Turn is a low brick. CO bets again and BTN folds.
- CO realized equity well by keeping initiative and forcing weaker hands to fold.

## Working Rules
- In position, medium-strength hands and draws are easier to play to showdown.
- Out of position, realization drops because you face pressure first and get less information.
- On dry boards, small bets often keep your range active at low risk.
- On wet boards, larger bets can deny equity and protect your current edge.
- Multiway pots lower realization for marginal hands, so tighten decisions and avoid bloating the pot.
