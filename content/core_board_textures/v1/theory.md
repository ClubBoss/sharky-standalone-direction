# Board Texture

## Key Idea
- Board texture describes how connected or static the community cards are.
- Dry boards change slowly and support smaller bets more often.
- Wet boards create more draws and stronger turn changes, so bigger bets or more checking become reasonable.

## Mini-Example
- On `K72` rainbow, the preflop raiser often keeps more strong hands and can use a small continuation bet.
- On `T98` two-tone, many draws are possible, so the caller can continue more often and a larger size makes more sense.
- Paired and monotone boards need extra care because medium hands can become fragile quickly.

## Working Rules
- Treat rainbow boards with wide gaps as dry until later cards add pressure.
- Treat connected or two-tone boards as wet because many turns can change the best hand.
- Use smaller bets more often on dry boards when your strong hands are still ahead.
- Use larger bets or more checking on wet boards when many draws can continue.
- Re-evaluate on the turn because a static flop can become dynamic fast.
