# River Fundamentals

## Key Idea
- The river is the final card, so there are no more draws to come.
- River decisions are final: you are usually choosing between betting for value, bluffing, checking, calling, or folding.
- Good river play is mostly about hand strength, bet size, and opponent tendencies.

## Definitions
- Definition: A value bet is a bet that expects calls from worse hands.
- Definition: A bluff is a bet that expects better hands to fold.

## Mini-Example
- CO bets flop and turn, BTN calls.
- River is a blank card that does not complete draws.
- CO now chooses between a larger value bet with very strong hands or a check with medium hands.
- BTN decides to call or fold based on price and how often CO can be bluffing.

## Working Rules
- Bet bigger on the river when you have very strong value and worse hands can still call.
- Use smaller value bets with medium-strong hands to avoid isolating against only better hands.
- Bluff less when your opponent is unlikely to fold and bluff more when their range is capped.
- Check medium showdown hands when a bet is unlikely to be called by worse.
- For bluff-catch decisions, compare pot odds to how many bluffs the opponent can realistically have.
