# Placement Test Vocabulary
🦈 Sharky: Before we dive into the tables, you need to speak poker.

[[Pocket Pair]]: When your two hole cards share the same rank (7-7 or A-A). These start strong because they already beat every single [[Pair]] on the board.

[[Offsuit]]: Two cards from different suits (a Heart and a Spade). They cannot make a [[Flush]] together, so treat them like mixed signals.

[[Suited]]: Two cards of the same suit. They have a higher chance of making a [[Flush]] and can sneak up on opponents.

[[Flush]]: Five cards of the exact same suit. It beats a [[Straight]], but loses to a [[Full House]].

[[Straight]]: Five cards in numerical order (5-6-7-8-9, for example). It beats Three of a Kind, but a [[Flush]] will still slow it down.

[[Stack]]: All the chips you have at the table. Keep track of your [[Stack]] as you fight pots and pressure.

[[Big Slick]]: A nickname for Ace-King (AK). One of the most famous hands and a real crowd-pleaser.

[[Kicker]]: The unmatched card in your hand. If two players have the same [[Pair]], the higher [[Kicker]] wins.
